## Executive Summary

This alignment analysis examines the gap between what the PCI Security Standards Council (PCI SSC) provides through its website/resources and what the industry actually needs. The analysis reveals a **fundamental misalignment** between PCI's comprehensive technical offerings and the industry's practical implementation needs, particularly around quality assurance, guidance clarity, and support for resource-constrained organizations.

**Key Alignment Finding:** PCI excels at **what to do** (standards development) but fails at **how to do it** (implementation support) and **ensuring it's done right** (quality control).

### 1.2 Core Alignment Problems

**Problem 1: Compliance Theater vs Real Security**
- **What PCI Provides:** Comprehensive compliance frameworks and assessment processes
- **What Industry Needs:** Actual security improvement and breach prevention
- **Gap:** Visa's Ellen Richey: "No compromised entity has yet been found to be in compliance with PCI DSS at the time of a breach"

**Problem 2: Technical Excellence vs Implementation Reality**
- **What PCI Provides:** World-class technical standards (15 comprehensive standards)
- **What Industry Needs:** Practical, step-by-step implementation guidance
- **Gap:** Industry feedback: "clear and actionable guidance" vs "theoretical framework"

**Problem 3: Universal Standards vs Resource Diversity**
- **What PCI Provides:** One-size-fits-all compliance requirements
- **What Industry Needs:** Scaled approaches for different organization sizes and capabilities
- **Gap:** Small merchants facing "truly impossible to achieve" compliance burdens

**Current State:**

- Limited practical examples and case studies
- Abstract documentation without actionable steps

**Industry Need:**
- Step-by-step implementation guides with real-world examples
- Technology-specific guidance for different platforms

**Recommendation:**
Create comprehensive "Implementation Playbook" with:

- Technology stack-specific implementation guides

- Interactive tools for requirement scoping and planning

#### **Gap 2: QSA Quality and Consistency Framework**

**Current State:**
- Basic QSA certification with minimal ongoing oversight
- No public performance metrics or quality indicators
- Limited mechanisms for addressing poor QSA performance

**Industry Need:**
- Rigorous QSA competency validation and ongoing monitoring

**Recommendation:**
Implement "QSA Excellence Program" featuring:

- Peer review processes for complex assessments
- Client feedback integration for QSA improvement

#### **Gap 3: Small Business Support Infrastructure**

**Current State:**
- Generic resources not scaled to small business capabilities
- Uniform requirements regardless of organization size or resources
- Limited support for resource-constrained implementation

**Industry Need:**
- Scaled compliance approaches based on business size and risk
- Cost-effective implementation tools and guidance

Develop "Small Business Compliance Pathway" including:
- Tiered compliance requirements based on transaction volume and risk

- Collaborative compliance programs for similar small businesses

## Conclusion

The alignment analysis reveals that while PCI Security Standards Council excels at standards development and global coordination, significant gaps exist in implementation support, quality assurance, and stakeholder service delivery. The organization's technical excellence is undermined by poor execution in practical support areas.

**The Core Alignment Challenge:** PCI has become a compliance organization when the industry needs a security effectiveness organization.

**The Path Forward:** Transform from a standards publisher to a comprehensive security implementation partner, with particular focus on quality assurance, practical guidance, and scaled support for diverse stakeholder needs.

**Success Indicator:** When industry sentiment shifts from "necessary evil" to "essential partner" in achieving measurable payment security improvements.
