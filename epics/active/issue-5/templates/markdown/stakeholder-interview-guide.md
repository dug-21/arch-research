## Interview Structure (60-90 minutes recommended)

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Introduction**:
"Thank you for your time today. We're conducting interviews to document our business processes comprehensively. Your insights are valuable for understanding how [Process Name] currently works and how it can be improved."

**Agenda Overview**:
1. Your role and experience (10 min)
2. Process overview and flow (20 min)
3. Operational details (20 min)
4. Challenges and pain points (15 min)
5. Improvement opportunities (10 min)
6. Wrap-up and next steps (5 min)

---

## Section 5: Improvement Opportunities (10 minutes)

### Ideal State

**Q26**: If you could wave a magic wand, how would you change this process?
- What would be different?
- What would the impact be?
- What's stopping this from happening now?

**Q27**: Are there tasks that could be automated?
- Which manual steps are repetitive?
- Which require minimal judgment?
- What would automation free you up to do?

**Q28**: How could technology better support this process?
- Better integration between systems?
- Real-time dashboards?
- Mobile access?
- Automated notifications?

### Benchmarking and Best Practices

**Q29**: Are you aware of how other organizations handle similar processes?
- Any best practices you'd like to adopt?
- Industry standards to follow?

**Q30**: Have there been recent changes or improvements to this process?
- What changed?
- What was the impact?
- What worked well/didn't work?

### Quick Wins

**Q31**: What's the one thing that would make the biggest difference to your daily work?
- Easiest improvement to implement?
- Highest impact change?

---

## Section 6: Wrap-up and Next Steps (5 minutes)

### Validation

**Q32**: Let me summarize what I heard... [Summarize key points]
- Is this accurate?
- Anything important I missed?
- Any corrections needed?

**Q33**: Who else should I talk to about this process?
- Other key participants?
- Subject matter experts?
- Downstream/upstream process owners?

**Q34**: Are there any documents, reports, or examples that would help me understand this better?
- Process documentation (current)?
- System screenshots?
- Sample reports or outputs?
- Error examples?

### Follow-up

**Q35**: Would you be available for a follow-up conversation if needed?
- Preferred contact method?
- Best times to reach you?

**Q36**: How would you like to be involved as we document and potentially improve this process?
- Review documentation?
- Participate in improvement initiatives?
- Provide ongoing feedback?

- [ ] Synthesize findings with other interviews

- [ ] Identify process improvement opportunities

- [ ] Share draft documentation with interviewee
- [ ] Incorporate feedback
- [ ] Schedule follow-up interviews if needed
- [ ] Begin improvement planning

## Interview Best Practices

### Before the Interview
- [ ] Research the process and interviewee background
- [ ] Prepare specific questions based on their role
- [ ] Set clear expectations for time and outcomes
- [ ] Test recording equipment if being used

### During the Interview
- [ ] Start on time and respect time boundaries
- [ ] Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions
- [ ] Probe for specific examples
- [ ] Take visual notes (flowcharts, diagrams)
- [ ] Confirm understanding throughout

### After the Interview
- [ ] Complete detailed notes immediately
- [ ] Send summary to interviewee for validation
- [ ] Schedule follow-ups promptly
- [ ] Share insights with project team

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- [ ] Don't lead the witness with your assumptions
- [ ] Don't skip operational details for "big picture" only
- [ ] Don't promise solutions during the interview
- [ ] Don't forget to ask about exceptions and edge cases
- [ ] Don't assume consistency across different stakeholders

---

*This guide is designed to be adapted based on the specific process and stakeholder role. Adjust questions and focus areas as appropriate.*
