# ASP.NET WebForms Architecture Assessment Research Charter

## Executive Summary

This research initiative focuses on conducting a comprehensive **architecture assessment** of the ASP.NET WebForms framework, specifically examining its current status, strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT) from an architectural perspective. This assessment explicitly **excludes implementation guidance** and instead concentrates on evaluating the framework's architectural fitness for existing applications.

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objective
Provide a comprehensive architectural assessment of ASP.NET WebForms to inform strategic decisions about existing applications using this framework.

### Specific Research Questions

1. **Current Status Assessment**
   - What is the current support status of ASP.NET WebForms in 2025?
   - What are Microsoft's official positions on WebForms lifecycle?
   - What is the framework's position in the technology landscape?

2. **SWOT Analysis (Architecture-Focused)**
   - **Strengths**: What architectural advantages does WebForms still offer?
   - **Weaknesses**: What are the critical architectural limitations?
   - **Opportunities**: What modernization paths exist for WebForms applications?
   - **Threats**: What risks face applications remaining on WebForms?

3. **Security Architecture Analysis**
   - What are the inherent security weaknesses in the WebForms architecture?
   - How do these weaknesses manifest in production applications?
   - What security gaps cannot be mitigated within the framework?

4. **Support and Lifecycle Analysis**
   - What are the specific EOL/EOS dates for ASP.NET 4.x and WebForms?
   - What is the current and future support model?
   - What are the implications for enterprise applications?

5. **Migration Architecture Patterns**
   - What are the recommended architectural migration paths?
   - What modernization strategies preserve business logic?
   - What are the architectural trade-offs of each path?

## Scope Definition

### In Scope
- Architectural analysis of ASP.NET WebForms framework
- Security vulnerability assessment at the framework level
- Support lifecycle and EOL/EOS timeline analysis
- SWOT analysis from an enterprise architecture perspective
- Migration path architectural patterns and strategies
- Current adoption trends and market position
- Architectural recommendations for 2025 and beyond

### Out of Scope
- How to build applications with WebForms
- Implementation tutorials or code examples
- Detailed programming guidance
- Feature-by-feature comparison with other frameworks
- Performance benchmarking of specific implementations
- Cost analysis of specific migration projects
