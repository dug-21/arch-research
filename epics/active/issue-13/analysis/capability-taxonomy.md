## Executive Summary

This comprehensive business capability taxonomy provides a standardized framework for categorizing and organizing business capabilities across organizations. The taxonomy is designed to be industry-agnostic while allowing for sector-specific customizations and extensions.

**Taxonomy Structure:**
- **4 Primary Domains:** Core, Supporting, Management, Foundation
- **16 Capability Groups:** Organized by functional area and purpose
- **64+ Individual Capabilities:** Specific business functions
- **200+ Sub-Capabilities:** Detailed capability components

---

## 1. Taxonomy Principles

### 1.1 Design Principles

#### **Principle 1: Hierarchical Structure**
- Clear parent-child relationships between capability levels
- Consistent decomposition across all domains
- Scalable from high-level strategic view to detailed operational view

#### **Principle 2: Functional Independence**
- Capabilities represent what is done, not how or who
- Technology and organizational structure independent
- Stable over time despite operational changes

#### **Principle 3: Comprehensive Coverage**
- Complete representation of organizational functions
- No gaps or overlaps in capability coverage
- Balanced level of detail across all areas

#### **Principle 4: Industry Adaptability**
- Core taxonomy applicable across industries
- Extension points for sector-specific capabilities
- Customization without losing standardization benefits

### 1.2 Classification Rules

#### **Domain Classification:**
- **Core:** Direct customer value creation
- **Supporting:** Enable core capabilities
- **Management:** Governance and oversight
- **Foundation:** Infrastructure and platforms

#### **Capability Naming:**
- Noun-based naming for stability
- Action-oriented when necessary for clarity
- Industry-standard terminology preferred
- Consistent linguistic patterns

## Conclusion

This comprehensive business capability taxonomy provides organizations with a structured framework for understanding and managing their business capabilities. The taxonomy balances standardization with flexibility, enabling organizations to benefit from best practices while adapting to their specific industry and organizational needs.

**Key Benefits:**
- **Standardized Language:** Common terminology for capability discussions
- **Comprehensive Coverage:** Complete organizational capability representation
- **Industry Adaptability:** Customizable for different sectors and organizations
- **Assessment Integration:** Direct alignment with maturity and performance assessments
- **Strategic Alignment:** Clear connection between capabilities and business outcomes

The taxonomy serves as the foundation for capability-based planning, assessment, and improvement initiatives, enabling organizations to make informed decisions about capability investments and development priorities.
