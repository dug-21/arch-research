# Strategic Planning Workshop Facilitation Guide

**Purpose:** Guide business leaders through structured strategic discussions using the 30 questions developed from non-profit association market research.

**Duration:** 2-4 hours (flexible based on objectives)
**Participants:** 5-12 business leaders (executive team, board members, senior managers)
**Materials Needed:** This guide, strategic questions document, research summaries, whiteboard/collaboration tools

---

## Pre-Workshop Preparation (1-2 Weeks Before)

### For the Facilitator

**1. Understand Your Audience**
- Review organizational history and current challenges
- Identify known areas of disagreement or misalignment
- Understand decision-making authority and culture
- Assess readiness for difficult conversations

**2. Select Questions**
- Don't use all 30 questions in one session
- Choose 8-12 questions that address specific objectives
- Sequence questions from broad to specific
- Mix comfortable and challenging topics

**3. Prepare Supporting Materials**
- Key data points from research documents
- Market trends relevant to organization
- Competitor examples (anonymized if needed)
- Visual aids (charts, frameworks, scenarios)

**4. Set Clear Objectives**
Choose ONE primary objective:
- **Alignment**: Build shared understanding of strategy
- **Decision**: Make specific strategic choice(s)
- **Discovery**: Identify gaps and opportunities
- **Planning**: Create actionable roadmap

### For Participants

**Required Pre-Reading (30-45 minutes):**
- Executive Summary
- Selected questions for the session
- One research or analysis document (assigned by topic area)

**Optional Deep Dive:**
- Full research section relevant to their role
- Vision documents for future thinking
- Technology roadmap for investment context

**Pre-Work Assignment:**
- "Write down your answer to Questions X, Y, Z before the session"
- Brings prepared perspectives and speeds discussion

## Workshop Structure Options

### Option 1: Strategy Alignment Session (3-4 Hours)

**Objective:** Build shared vision and identify strategic priorities

**Agenda:**
1. **Opening (15 min)**
   - Context setting: Why we're here
   - Ground rules and objectives
   - Brief research highlights

2. **Market Position Block (45 min)**
   - Questions 1-5 from Market Position & Differentiation
   - Focus: Where do we stand and why?
   - Output: SWOT or positioning map

3. **Future Vision Block (45 min)**
   - Questions 21-25 from Innovation & Future Readiness
   - Focus: Where are we going?
   - Output: 3-year vision statement

4. **Break (15 min)**

5. **Capability Assessment Block (45 min)**
   - Questions 16-20 from Organizational Capabilities
   - Focus: What do we need to build?
   - Output: Capability gap analysis

6. **Priority Setting Block (45 min)**
   - Questions 6-10 from Technology Strategy
   - Focus: Where do we invest?
   - Output: Top 3-5 strategic priorities

7. **Action Planning (30 min)**
   - Assign owners to priorities
   - Define 30/60/90-day milestones
   - Establish metrics and review cadence

### Option 2: Decision-Making Session (2 Hours)

**Objective:** Make specific strategic decision (e.g., AI investment, partnership, market expansion)

**Agenda:**
1. **Frame the Decision (15 min)**
   - What are we deciding?
   - What are the options?
   - What are the constraints?

2. **Explore Context (30 min)**
   - 3-4 targeted questions that illuminate the decision
   - Present relevant research data
   - Discuss implications of each option

3. **Break (10 min)**

4. **Structured Debate (40 min)**
   - Divide group: half argues for option A, half for option B
   - 10 min preparation per side
   - 10 min presentation per side
   - 10 min rebuttal and discussion

5. **Decision & Planning (25 min)**
   - Decision-maker (CEO/board) makes call
   - Immediate next steps identified
   - Success criteria defined

### Option 3: Discovery Workshop (2.5 Hours)

**Objective:** Identify gaps, challenges, and opportunities without pressure to decide

**Agenda:**
1. **Individual Reflection (20 min)**
   - Participants write responses to 5-6 selected questions
   - Silent, independent thinking time
   - Builds psychological safety for dissent

2. **Small Group Discussions (40 min)**
   - Break into 2-3 groups of 3-4 people
   - Each group discusses different question set
   - Groups prepare summary for full team

3. **Share Back (30 min)**
   - Each group presents key insights (7-10 min each)
   - Questions and clarifications
   - Note patterns and disagreements

4. **Break (10 min)**

5. **Full Group Synthesis (40 min)**
   - Facilitator highlights themes
   - Group identifies top 5 insights
   - Discuss implications for strategy

6. **Next Steps (10 min)**
   - Assign research or analysis tasks
   - Schedule follow-up session(s)
   - Define what "done" looks like

## Facilitation Techniques

### Starting Strong

**Opening Frame:**
> "We've invested in comprehensive research on the non-profit association market—current reality and future vision through 2032. Today's questions are designed to challenge our assumptions, surface hidden disagreements, and build shared understanding. There are no predetermined answers. Your honest perspectives, even if they disagree with leadership or each other, are exactly what we need."

**Ground Rules:**
1. **Candor over consensus**: Say what you think, not what's comfortable
2. **Evidence over opinion**: Reference research and data when possible
3. **Future over past**: Focus on what we should do, not what we did
4. **Questions over answers**: It's okay to leave questions unresolved
5. **Respect dissent**: Disagreement drives better decisions

### Managing Discussion

**When Discussion Goes Off Track:**
- "That's an important point. Let's capture it [on parking lot] and return to..."
- "How does this relate to the question: [restate question]?"
- "We have 10 minutes left on this topic. What's most important to resolve?"

**When Group Avoids Difficult Topics:**
- "I notice we haven't addressed the elephant in the room: [name it]"
- "The research suggests [uncomfortable truth]. How should we think about this?"
- "If our competitors were in this room, what would they say we're avoiding?"

**When Strong Personalities Dominate:**
- "We've heard from several people. Who hasn't spoken yet?"
- "Let's go around the table—30 seconds each on this question"
- "I'd like to hear the contrarian view. Who disagrees with what's been said?"

**When Discussion Gets Stuck:**
- "Let's vote: How many believe X? How many believe Y?"
- "What data or research would help us resolve this?"
- "What would [trusted advisor/industry expert] say about this?"
- "Let's table this and revisit after we discuss Question X"

### Question-Specific Facilitation Tips

**For Market Position Questions (1-5):**
- Push for specificity: "Why do clients ACTUALLY choose us?"
- Challenge assumptions: "Is this still true today?"
- Force prioritization: "If you could only keep ONE advantage, which?"

**For Technology Strategy Questions (6-10):**
- Connect to financials: "What's the ROI model?"
- Address risk: "What's the worst case if we don't invest?"
- Timeline reality: "When must this decision be made?"

**For Client Needs Questions (11-15):**
- Use empathy: "Put yourself in client's shoes..."
- Validate with data: "Does our research support this?"
- Quantify: "What % of clients need this today vs. 2027?"

**For Organizational Capabilities Questions (16-20):**
- Be honest about gaps: "What CAN'T we do today?"
- Distinguish must-have from nice-to-have
- Consider build vs. buy vs. partner

**For Innovation Questions (21-25):**
- Balance creativity with reality
- Use confidence levels: "Is this high, medium, or low confidence?"
- Connect to today: "What's the first step?"

**For Security & Trust Questions (26-30):**
- Don't let technical jargon hide issues
- Connect to client trust and brand
- Discuss incident response readiness

## Consensus-Building Techniques

### Fist-to-Five Voting
After discussion, ask: "On a scale of 0 (fist) to 5 fingers, how much do you support this direction?"
- **5 fingers**: Strongly support
- **4 fingers**: Support
- **3 fingers**: Can live with it
- **2 fingers**: Have concerns but won't block
- **1 finger**: Serious concerns, want more discussion
- **Fist**: Block—cannot support

**If you get mostly 3-5:** Move forward with documented concerns
**If you get multiple 1-2:** More discussion needed
**If you get a fist:** That person must explain why; group must address

### Two-Column Technique
Divide whiteboard:
| We Believe (Consensus) | We're Not Sure (Needs Work) |
|------------------------|------------------------------|

Move statements from "not sure" to "believe" as consensus builds.

### Dot Voting for Priorities
When choosing between multiple options:
1. List all options on wall/board
2. Give each person 3-5 dots
3. Place dots on top priorities
4. Discuss results and refine

### Decision-Making Frameworks

**For Strategic Decisions:**
```
Impact (1-10) × Feasibility (1-10) = Priority Score
```

**For Investment Decisions:**
```
Expected ROI × Strategic Fit × Risk Level = Investment Score
```

**For Capability Building:**
```
Client Need × Competitive Advantage × Build Feasibility = Priority
```

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: "We don't have enough information"
**Solution:**
- Identify specific data needed
- Assign research task with deadline
- Make provisional decision: "If we learn X, we'll do Y"
- Use confidence levels: "We're 70% confident, which is enough to proceed"

### Challenge: "This is just theoretical—our business is different"
**Solution:**
- Acknowledge uniqueness: "You're right, every business is different"
- Find applicable elements: "Which parts of this research DO apply?"
- Use scenarios: "If our largest client asked for this, what would we do?"
- Test assumptions: "How would we validate whether this matters for us?"

### Challenge: "We've tried this before and it failed"
**Solution:**
- Honor the past: "That experience matters—what did we learn?"
- Identify what's different: "What's changed since then?"
- Ask: "If we did it again, what would we do differently?"
- Consider: "What's the cost of NOT trying again?"

### Challenge: Leadership Already Has the Answer
**Solution:**
- Leader frames at start: "I have a perspective, but I want to hear yours first"
- Leader speaks last: Listens to all input before sharing view
- Leader acknowledges disagreement: "I hear you disagree—help me understand why"
- Leader decides transparently: "Here's what I'm deciding and why, even though some disagree"

### Challenge: Team is Conflict-Averse
**Solution:**
- Normalize disagreement: "We NEED different views to make good decisions"
- Assign roles: "Who wants to play devil's advocate?"
- Anonymous input: Use anonymous survey/votes before discussion
- Reframe: "Disagreement now prevents mistakes later"

## Follow-Up Activities

### Immediate (Within 48 Hours)
- [ ] Send session notes with key decisions and action items
- [ ] Distribute responsibility assignments
- [ ] Share any parking lot items that need follow-up
- [ ] Schedule next session if needed

### Short-Term (Within 2 Weeks)
- [ ] Complete any assigned research or analysis
- [ ] Create detailed project plans for priorities
- [ ] Communicate decisions to broader organization
- [ ] Begin executing quick wins

### Medium-Term (Within 30 Days)
- [ ] Review progress on action items
- [ ] Adjust plans based on new information
- [ ] Schedule regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly)
- [ ] Celebrate early successes

### Long-Term (Ongoing)
- [ ] Revisit strategic questions quarterly
- [ ] Update market research annually
- [ ] Adjust strategy as market evolves
- [ ] Measure outcomes against goals

## Virtual Workshop Adaptations

**Technology Tools:**
- **Collaboration**: Miro, Mural, Google Jamboard
- **Voting**: Mentimeter, Slido, Poll Everywhere
- **Breakouts**: Zoom breakout rooms with clear instructions
- **Documentation**: Shared Google Doc with live note-taking

**Virtual-Specific Tips:**
- Send pre-work 3-5 days in advance (higher no-show risk)
- Start with "camera on" norm
- Use chat for questions/reactions
- Take breaks every 60-75 minutes (screen fatigue)
- Assign someone to monitor chat and nonverbal cues
- Use visual tools more heavily (harder to maintain engagement)

## Sample Session Plans

### Sample 1: "AI Strategy Decision" (2 Hours)

**Participants:** CEO, CTO, CFO, VP Sales, VP Operations
**Decision:** Whether to invest $500K in AI capabilities in 2026

**Question Selection:**
- Q7: Investment priority (AI vs. other options)
- Q12: Client AI needs timeline
- Q17: AI capability gaps
- Q22: Competitive positioning with AI
- Q27: AI security and privacy considerations

**Agenda:**
1. Frame decision & options (10 min)
2. Questions 12 & 17: Client need + our gaps (25 min)
3. Questions 7 & 22: Investment case + competitive impact (25 min)
4. Break (10 min)
5. Question 27: Risk and compliance (20 min)
6. Decision discussion (20 min)
7. Next steps & assignments (10 min)

**Expected Outcome:** Go/No-go decision with implementation plan

### Sample 2: "3-Year Vision Alignment" (4 Hours)

**Participants:** Full leadership team (10 people)
**Objective:** Build shared 3-year vision and strategic priorities

**Question Selection:**
- Q1, Q2: Market position
- Q6, Q7, Q10: Technology strategy
- Q11, Q13: Client evolution
- Q18, Q19: Organizational capabilities
- Q21, Q23: Innovation direction
- Q28: Security positioning

**Agenda:**
1. Opening & context (15 min)
2. Individual reflection (20 min)
3. Small group: Market & clients (Q1, Q2, Q11, Q13) (30 min)
4. Share back (20 min)
5. Break (15 min)
6. Small group: Capabilities & innovation (Q18, Q19, Q21, Q23) (30 min)
7. Share back (20 min)
8. Lunch (45 min)
9. Full group: Technology & security (Q6, Q7, Q10, Q28) (45 min)
10. Vision synthesis (30 min)
11. Priority identification (30 min)
12. Close & next steps (10 min)

**Expected Outcome:** Written 3-year vision, top 5 priorities, action plan

## Facilitator Checklist

**Week Before:**
- [ ] Confirm objectives with sponsor
- [ ] Select and sequence questions
- [ ] Prepare supporting materials
- [ ] Send pre-work to participants
- [ ] Book space and arrange technology
- [ ] Prepare facilitation aids (worksheets, frameworks)

**Day Before:**
- [ ] Review participant pre-work submissions
- [ ] Adjust agenda based on responses
- [ ] Prepare opening and closing remarks
- [ ] Test technology (especially for virtual)
- [ ] Print materials if in-person

**Day Of:**
- [ ] Arrive early to set up space
- [ ] Test A/V and collaboration tools
- [ ] Set up note-taking system
- [ ] Put phones away (model behavior)
- [ ] Take deep breath—you've got this!

**During Session:**
- [ ] Watch time rigorously
- [ ] Track parking lot items
- [ ] Note decisions and action items
- [ ] Observe body language and engagement
- [ ] Adjust agenda if needed

**After Session:**
- [ ] Document key outcomes immediately
- [ ] Send notes within 48 hours
- [ ] Follow up on action items
- [ ] Solicit feedback from participants
- [ ] Plan next steps

## Success Metrics

**Strong Session Indicators:**
- Healthy debate and disagreement
- Specific examples and data referenced
- "I changed my mind about..." statements
- Concrete action items with owners
- Parking lot items captured (not resolved mid-session)
- Participants energized, not exhausted
- Follow-through on commitments after session

**Weak Session Indicators:**
- Rapid consensus without discussion
- Vague generalities and platitudes
- Dominant voices, silent participants
- Unresolved conflicts swept under rug
- No clear next steps or owners
- Participants check out mentally
- No follow-through after session

---

**Final Thought:** The best strategic discussions are messy, challenging, and sometimes uncomfortable. Embrace the difficulty—it means you're addressing real issues. Your job as facilitator is to create safety for candor, structure for productivity, and urgency for action.

Good luck! 🚀
