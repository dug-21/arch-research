# Simplified Working Backwards Program
## For Non-Technical Business Leaders

**Key Simplification:** Participants answer strategic questions (homework), you synthesize into press releases, then work together to refine and answer FAQs.

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks | 2-3 sessions
**Total Participant Time:** ~12-15 hours (vs. 22-25 in original)

---

## 🎯 WHY THIS APPROACH IS BETTER FOR YOUR AUDIENCE

### Original Approach (Too Heavy)
- Participants write full press releases (8-10 hours homework)
- Participants write individual FAQs
- Requires strong writing skills
- Risk of low quality submissions
- Heavy homework burden

### Simplified Approach (Realistic)
- Participants answer strategic questions (3-4 hours homework)
- You synthesize answers into draft press releases
- FAQs done together in session
- Leverages your synthesis skills
- Reasonable homework burden

---

## 📋 COMPLETE PROGRAM STRUCTURE

### Session 1: Introduction + Assignment (2.5 hours, In-Person)
**What Happens:**
1. Present Virtual Inc. provocative press release
2. Teach press release anatomy
3. Discuss reactions and possibilities
4. Assign homework: Strategic question worksheet

**Output:** Homework assigned

---

### Homework: Strategic Question Worksheet (3-4 hours)
**What Participants Do:**
- Answer 12-15 strategic questions
- Reflect on member needs, capabilities, vision
- Provide concrete examples and metrics where possible

**What You Do:**
- Collect responses
- Synthesize into 2-4 draft press releases representing different directions
- Prepare to present these in Session 2

---

### Session 2: Draft Review + Direction Selection (3 hours, In-Person)
**What Happens:**
1. You present 2-4 synthesized press release drafts
2. Group discusses each one
3. Choose best elements from each
4. Create ONE consensus press release together
5. Begin answering FAQs as a group

**Output:** Single 2030 press release + started FAQ

---

### Session 3 (Optional): FAQ Completion + Strategy Development (2-3 hours)
**What Happens:**
1. Finish FAQ together
2. Convert press release into Strategy-on-a-Page
3. Build 90-day action plan

**Output:** Complete strategy package

---

## 🔧 YOUR SYNTHESIS WORK (Between Homework and Session 2)

### Step 1: Collect and Organize Responses (1 hour)

**Create synthesis spreadsheet:**
| Question | Person 1 | Person 2 | Person 3 | ... | Themes |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|-----|--------|

**Look for:**
- Common problems identified
- Convergent solutions
- Outlier/unique ideas
- Strong headlines
- Compelling quotes
- Specific metrics

### Step 2: Identify Strategic Directions (2 hours)

**Based on responses, identify 2-4 distinct directions:**

**Example Directions:**
1. **AI-Powered Personalization Direction**
   - Focuses on personalized member experiences
   - AI career advisors, learning paths
   - Mentioned by 5 of 8 participants

2. **Platform/Ecosystem Direction**
   - Becomes "Association OS"
   - White-label platform for multiple associations
   - Mentioned by 3 of 8 participants

3. **Generational Transformation Direction**
   - Mobile-first, community-driven
   - Micro-credentials, instant gratification
   - Mentioned by 4 of 8 participants

4. **Revenue Model Innovation Direction**
   - Subscription tiers, data-as-a-service
   - Move from dues to value-based pricing
   - Mentioned by 2 of 8 participants (outlier but compelling)

### Step 3: Write 2-4 Draft Press Releases (4-6 hours)

**For each direction, write 2-3 page press release using:**
- Best headlines from participants
- Most compelling problem statements
- Strongest solution ideas
- Actual quotes from participants (with permission)
- Metrics that appeared multiple times
- Synthesized FAQ addressing concerns raised

**Template for Each Draft:**

```

[Synthesize solution ideas]

[Synthesize outcomes and metrics]
```

## 📊 SESSION 2: DRAFT REVIEW + DIRECTION SELECTION (3 Hours)

### Agenda

**0:00-0:10 | Opening (10 min)**
- Thank everyone for homework
- "I read all your responses and synthesized into draft press releases"
- "Today we review, discuss, and choose our direction"

**0:10-0:30 | Synthesis Overview (20 min)**
- Present what you heard across all responses
- Common themes
- Outlier ideas
- Interesting tensions/trade-offs

**0:30-1:45 | Present Draft Press Releases (75 min)**

**For each draft (15-20 min per draft if 4 drafts):**
1. **Read the draft aloud** (5 min)
   - Headline
   - Problem section
   - Solution section
   - Key quotes and metrics

2. **"What You Contributed"** (2 min)
   - Point out whose ideas are in this draft
   - Give credit
   - "This builds on [Name's] idea about X"

3. **Discussion** (8-10 min)
   - What excites you about this direction?
   - What concerns you?
   - What's missing?
   - Is this achievable by 2030?

**Capture reactions on whiteboard**

**1:45-2:00 | BREAK (15 min)**

**2:00-2:40 | Choose Direction + Refine (40 min)**

**Activity: Dot Voting (10 min)**
- Everyone gets 3 dots
- Vote for drafts that:
  - Most excite you
  - Seem most achievable
  - Would most differentiate us

**Discussion (20 min)**
- What's leading?
- What elements from other drafts should we integrate?
- What needs strengthening?

**Decision (10 min)**
- Choose ONE direction (or synthesis of 2 if very similar)
- Document decision
- "This is our 2030 vision"

**2:40-3:00 | Begin FAQ Together (20 min)**

**Collaborative FAQ Exercise:**
"Now let's answer the hard questions together."

**Pick 3-4 critical FAQs to start:**
1. What would this cost and how do we fund it?
2. What capabilities must we build?
3. How do we bring stakeholders along?
4. What are biggest risks?

**For each FAQ:**
- Brainstorm together (5 min per question)
- Capture answers on whiteboard
- Assign follow-up research if needed

**Close:**
- "Great progress. We have our 2030 vision."
- "Next session we finish FAQs and build execution plan"
- OR "Let's continue right now if we have energy"

✅ Faster (2-3 weeks vs. 4-5 weeks)
✅ More realistic for busy executives
✅ Higher completion rates (simpler homework)
✅ Better group alignment (working together not competing)
✅ Produces same quality output with less burden
