# Working Backwards Strategic Engagement Program
## AMC + Client Strategy Development Using Amazon's Working Backwards Method

**Updated Approach:** January 2025
**Philosophy:** Start with the customer outcome (2030 press release), work backwards to strategy
**Timeline:** 4-5 weeks | 4 core sessions
**Key Deliverable:** 2030 Press Release + FAQ + Strategy-on-a-Page + 90-Day Action Plan

---

## 🎯 PROGRAM PHILOSOPHY

### Why Working Backwards?

**Traditional Strategy (Inside-Out):**
1. Assess current capabilities
2. Identify what we can build
3. Create strategy around our strengths
4. Hope customers want it

**Working Backwards (Outside-In):**
1. Define what customers will love in 2030
2. Work backwards to what must be true
3. Build capabilities to deliver that future
4. Start with customer delight, not current constraints

### Amazon's Core Principle

> "We start with the customer and work backwards. We listen to customers, but we also invent on their behalf."

**For this program:**
- Customer = Association members (end users)
- Invention = What AMC + Client must create together
- Press Release = Concrete articulation of 2030 success
- FAQ = Honest assessment of how to get there

#### PART 4: Anatomy of a Press Release (45 min) | 1:25-2:10

**Transition:**
> "Your homework is to write YOUR version of this press release for 2030. But first, let me teach you what makes a great press release and why Amazon uses this method."

**Teaching Module: Why Press Releases?**

**1. Forces Customer-Centric Thinking (5 min)**
- Can't write press release without knowing customer value
- If headline doesn't excite customers, idea isn't good enough
- "Would a member actually care about this announcement?"

**2. Forces Concrete Specificity (5 min)**
- No vague "strategic themes" allowed
- Must have numbers, dates, outcomes
- Test: "Could you announce this publicly with pride?"

**3. Forces Hard Questions Early (5 min)**
- FAQ reveals what you don't know
- Better to discover blockers on paper than in execution
- "If we can't answer the FAQ, we're not ready"

**Anatomy of a Working Backwards Press Release (30 min)**

**Show structure on screen:**

```
PRESS RELEASE ANATOMY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HEADLINE
• One sentence, specific, quantified
• Must pass "Would a customer care?" test
• Example: "100 AI-Augmented Associations Achieve 3X Engagement"

SUBHEADLINE
• Expand on headline, add context
• Who benefits? What changes?
• Example: "New platform helps small associations compete with billion-dollar orgs"

LOCATION & DATE
• Where/when is this announcement?
• Usually 3-5 years in future
• Example: "BOSTON, MA - December 15, 2030"

THE PROBLEM/CRISIS SECTION
• What problem are we solving?
• Why does it matter NOW?
• What's at stake if we don't solve it?
• Make it visceral, not abstract
• Example: "60% of associations lost 30% of members by 2028"

THE SOLUTION/TRANSFORMATION SECTION
• What did we build/create/launch?
• How does it solve the problem?
• What's different about our approach?
• Specific capabilities, not buzzwords
• Example: "Association OS combines AI insights + predictive certification + autonomous events"

THE CUSTOMER IMPACT SECTION
• What changes for customers?
• What can they do now they couldn't before?
• Quantified benefits
• Example: "From quarterly check-ins to daily engagement"

CUSTOMER QUOTE
• What would a delighted customer say?
• Must sound authentic, not marketing-speak
• Should capture emotional + functional value
• Example: "We went from 80% admin/20% value to 100% strategic focus"

COMPANY QUOTE (Your CEO/Leader)
• Strategic vision statement
• Why this matters for industry
• What's your unique insight?
• Example: "We stopped being back-office and became intelligence layer"

CALL TO ACTION
• How can customers learn more?
• What's the next step?
• Keep it simple
• Example: "Visit virtualinc.com/2030 for assessment"
```

**Walk Through Each Section Using Virtual Inc. Example:**
- Point to each section in the example
- Explain why it works
- Show how it could be different/better

#### PART 5: FAQ Teaching (20 min) | 2:10-2:30

**Transition:**
> "The press release is the vision. The FAQ is the reality check. This is where strategy actually lives."

**Why FAQ Matters:**

The FAQ forces you to answer:
1. **How did you get there?** (Capabilities, investments)
2. **Why should we believe you?** (Proof points, credibility)
3. **What could go wrong?** (Risks, competition)
4. **What will it cost?** (Resources, trade-offs)
5. **Who will resist?** (Change management, politics)

**FAQ Categories to Address:**

**Show on screen:**

```
FAQ CATEGORIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. CAPABILITIES & INVESTMENT
   Q: What capabilities did we need to build?
   Q: How much did this cost?
   Q: How did we fund it?

2. CHANGE MANAGEMENT
   Q: How did we convince stakeholders?
   Q: How did we handle staff concerns?
   Q: What about resistant boards?

3. COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS
   Q: What about competitors?
   Q: What prevents others from copying us?
   Q: What's our sustainable advantage?

4. CUSTOMER ADOPTION
   Q: Why would customers choose this?
   Q: What about customers who want traditional approach?
   Q: How did we prove value?

5. RISKS & THREATS
   Q: What could make this obsolete?
   Q: What are the biggest risks?
   Q: What could go wrong?

6. OPERATIONAL DETAILS
   Q: How does this actually work?
   Q: What's the implementation timeline?
   Q: What infrastructure is required?
```

**Example FAQ Walkthrough:**

Pick 2-3 FAQs from Virtual Inc. and analyze:
- "How did Virtual convince traditional boards?" → Change management strategy
- "What capabilities did Virtual need?" → Specific investments ($25M, 40 engineers)
- "What prevents tech giants from crushing this?" → Defensible moat (domain expertise)

**Key Point:**
> "If you can't answer these questions, your press release is fantasy. The FAQ is where you prove you've thought it through."

#### PART 7: Q&A & Close (10 min) | 2:50-3:00

**Common Questions:**

**Q: "Do we each write our own press release, or work together?"**
A: Each person writes their own. We want multiple visions, not consensus yet. You'll present in Session 2 and we'll identify the best ideas.

**Q: "How realistic should this be?"**
A: Bold but believable. It should stretch us, but not be science fiction. Ask: "Could this realistically happen by 2030 if we committed fully?"

**Q: "Can we work on this together between sessions?"**
A: Absolutely. But everyone submits their own version. Collaboration enriches, but we need individual perspectives.

**Q: "What if I don't know the answers to the FAQ?"**
A: That's the point! The FAQ surfaces what you DON'T know. Write "This would require research" or "We'd need to validate X." Honesty is valued.

**Q: "Should AMC and Client write different press releases?"**
A: You can write from different perspectives if you want. But ideally, the press release is SHARED success. What do AMC + Client achieve TOGETHER for members?

### Facilitator Tips for Session 1

- Don't defend or apologize for ambition

- Use different modalities (present, discuss, teach, assign)
- End on inspiration, not intimidation
