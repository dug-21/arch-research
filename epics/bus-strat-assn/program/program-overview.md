# Strategic Business Engagement Program
## Association Management Company & Key Client

**Date:** January 2025
**Duration:** 3-4 Weeks Total (4 Sessions + Homework)
**Participants:** AMC Leadership + Client Leadership
**Objective:** Develop actionable strategy for 2025-2030 with urgency and clarity

---

## CRITICAL FEEDBACK ON YOUR PROPOSED APPROACH

### What's Working ✅

1. **Homework with Thought-Provoking Questions**: EXCELLENT. Pre-work creates psychological commitment, ensures people show up prepared, and surfaces disagreements privately before public discussion.

2. **2030 Assumptions Framework**: SMART. Forces concrete future thinking instead of abstract "vision statements." Grounds discussion in verifiable trends.

3. **Strategy-on-a-Page**: PERFECT for this audience. Executives need visual, sharable artifacts they can reference and communicate.

4. **Sense of Urgency**: CRITICAL. Prolonged strategy processes lose momentum and become academic exercises.

### What Needs Adjustment ⚠️

1. **TWO Separate Homework Packets is WRONG**
   - **Problem**: You'll create divergent mental models BEFORE the first session
   - **Risk**: AMC thinks about tech, client thinks about members, they arrive at Session 1 misaligned
   - **Better**: ONE shared packet with role-specific reflection questions at the end
   - **Why**: Shared context = shared language = productive discussion

2. **"Send homework and then initiate program" is BACKWARDS**
   - **Problem**: People won't do homework without knowing WHY
   - **Better**: Brief kickoff call (30 min) → homework → Session 1
   - **Why**: Personal commitment in live setting dramatically improves homework completion

3. **Number of Sessions is UNDEFINED**
   - **Problem**: "Can't be a lot" is vague and anxiety-inducing
   - **Better**: Exactly 4 sessions + deliverables, no more
   - **Why**: Defined scope prevents scope creep and calendar chaos

4. **Missing the REAL First Step**
   - **Problem**: You're starting with "future thinking" when alignment on CURRENT STATE is unclear
   - **Better**: Session 1 must establish shared understanding of TODAY before discussing tomorrow
   - **Why**: You can't agree on direction if you disagree on location

### What's MISSING (Critical Gaps) 🚨

1. **No Conflict Resolution Plan**
   - AMC and client will have different priorities
   - Need explicit process for handling disagreement
   - Without it, nice discussions → no decisions

2. **No Decision Authority Framework**
   - Who decides if AMC and client disagree?
   - What happens if homework reveals fundamental misalignment?
   - Clarity prevents frustration

3. **No "What Good Looks Like" Definition**
   - How will you know this program succeeded?
   - Need concrete outcomes, not "better alignment"

4. **No Follow-Through Mechanism**
   - Strategy documents without execution = expensive decoration
   - Need 30/60/90-day accountability built into program

## RECOMMENDED PROGRAM STRUCTURE

### Program Overview

**Total Timeline:** 3-4 weeks
**Total Time Investment:** ~15-18 hours per participant
**Format:** Virtual + In-Person Hybrid

### The 4-Session Framework

| Session | Duration | Format | Outcome |
|---------|----------|--------|---------|
| **Kickoff** | 30 min | Virtual | Commitment + homework assignment |
| **Session 1** | 3 hours | In-Person | Shared understanding of current state |
| **Session 2** | 2 hours | Virtual | 2030 vision + strategic themes |
| **Session 3** | 3 hours | In-Person | Strategy-on-a-Page draft |
| **Session 4** | 2 hours | Virtual | Final strategy + 90-day action plan |

**Total In-Person Time:** 6 hours (2 sessions)
**Total Virtual Time:** 4.5 hours (3 sessions)
**Homework Time:** ~5-8 hours total across 3 assignments

## SESSION-BY-SESSION BREAKDOWN

### KICKOFF CALL (30 minutes, Virtual, Week 0)

**Attendees:** All participants (AMC + Client leadership)

**Agenda:**
1. Why we're doing this (5 min)
2. Program structure overview (10 min)
3. Homework assignment + expectations (10 min)
4. Q&A and commitment (5 min)

**Critical Elements:**
- **Personal ask from senior leader**: "I need your best thinking. Please complete the homework."
- **Explain the stakes**: "We're at an inflection point. This shapes our next 5 years."
- **Set norms**: Candor, data over opinion, future over past

**Output:** Verbal commitment to complete homework

---

### HOMEWORK PACKAGE #1 (Due 1 week after kickoff)

**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours
**Format:** Shared packet for both AMC and Client
**Delivery:** Digital workbook (Google Doc or PDF with fillable fields)

**Part A: 2030 Assumptions (Everyone Reads, Everyone Reflects)**

*Section 1: Macro Trends (With Verified Links)*
- Future of work and remote collaboration
- AI adoption in professional associations
- Member expectations evolution (Gen Z + Millennials)
- Regulatory environment (data privacy, AI governance)
- Economic outlook for associations

*Section 2: Industry-Specific Trends*
- Association management technology
- Member engagement patterns
- Revenue model evolution
- Competition and consolidation

**Part B: Reflection Questions**

*Shared Questions (Everyone Answers):*
1. What trends do you believe are INEVITABLE by 2030, regardless of what we do?
2. What trends are UNCERTAIN and could go multiple ways?
3. If we continue our current path without strategic changes, where will we be in 2030?
4. What would need to be true for us to THRIVE (not just survive) in 2030?

*Role-Specific Questions (Answer Only Your Section):*

**For AMC Leadership:**
5. What capabilities must we build by 2028 that we don't have today?
6. What client needs are we currently NOT meeting well?
7. If we could change ONE thing about how we serve this client, what would it be?

**For Client Leadership:**
8. What member needs are we failing to address with current technology/services?
9. What do our best members expect that we're not delivering?
10. If we had unlimited budget, what would we change about our member experience?

**Part C: Provocative Scenarios (Choose 2 to Respond To)**

*Scenario 1: AI Displacement*
> By 2028, AI agents can handle 70% of routine member inquiries, event registrations, and content recommendations. How does this change what we do for members? What becomes MORE important?

*Scenario 2: Generational Shift*
> By 2030, 60% of your members are Millennials and Gen Z who expect mobile-first, instant, personalized experiences like they get from Netflix and Amazon. Your current member experience feels "clunky" to them. What changes?

*Scenario 3: Revenue Pressure*
> Economic downturn in 2026-2027 causes 25% of members to cancel or downgrade. You must cut costs AND improve value simultaneously. What stays, what goes, what transforms?

*Scenario 4: Competitive Disruption*
> A well-funded tech company launches a direct-to-member platform in your space with AI-powered networking, micro-credentials, and viral growth. They're not an "association" but they're solving member needs. How do you respond?

**Deliverable:** Submit written responses 2 days before Session 1

### SESSION 1: Current State Alignment (3 hours, In-Person, Week 1-2)

**Attendees:** All participants
**Pre-Read:** Everyone's homework responses (distributed 1 day before)

**Objectives:**
1. Build shared understanding of current capabilities and gaps
2. Surface and resolve factual disagreements
3. Identify strategic themes that emerge from homework

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Homework Share-Out (60 min)**
- Small group discussions (mixed AMC + Client)
- Share 2030 assumptions and scenarios
- Capture themes on whiteboard

**Part 2: Current State Assessment (60 min)**
- What's working well (keep doing)
- What's not working (stop or fix)
- What's missing (start doing)
- Use data from previous research to validate/challenge

**Part 3: Strategic Themes Identification (45 min)**
- Synthesize homework + current state discussion
- Identify 4-6 strategic themes (examples below)
- Dot voting to prioritize top 4

**Possible Strategic Themes:**
- AI-Powered Member Experience
- Revenue Diversification
- Operational Excellence & Automation
- Data-Driven Decision Making
- Mobile-First Digital Transformation
- Security & Trust as Differentiator

**Part 4: Homework Assignment #2 (15 min)**
- Assign theme deep-dives
- Provide research materials

**Output:**
- Documented current state consensus
- 4 prioritized strategic themes
- Theme deep-dive assignments

---

### HOMEWORK PACKAGE #2 (Due 4 days after Session 1)

**Estimated Time:** 1.5-2 hours
**Format:** Theme-based research and reflection
**Assignment:** Each participant picks 1-2 themes to research

**Instructions:**
1. Review research materials provided for your theme(s)
2. Find 2-3 additional examples (companies, associations, case studies)
3. Answer the strategic questions below for your theme
4. Prepare 5-minute presentation for Session 2

**Questions for Each Theme:**

1. **Vision Statement**: What does "great" look like for this theme in 2030?
2. **Current Gap**: On a scale of 1-10, where are we today? What's the evidence?
3. **Quick Wins**: What could we do in 90 days to move the needle?
4. **Big Bets**: What 1-2 year investments would transform this?
5. **Success Metrics**: How will we measure progress?
6. **Risks**: What could prevent us from succeeding?

**Deliverable:** 1-slide summary per theme + notes for discussion

---

### SESSION 2: 2030 Vision + Theme Development (2 hours, Virtual, Week 2-3)

**Attendees:** All participants
**Pre-Read:** Theme research slides (distributed 1 day before)

**Objectives:**
1. Develop detailed vision for each strategic theme
2. Identify interdependencies between themes
3. Begin prioritization and sequencing

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Theme Presentations (60 min)**
- 5 min presentation per theme (4 themes = 20 min)
- 10 min discussion per theme (40 min total)
- Capture vision statements and metrics

**Part 2: Integration Discussion (40 min)**
- How do themes connect? (e.g., AI needs data, data needs security)
- What's the logical sequence? (what enables what?)
- What trade-offs must we make? (can't do everything)

**Part 3: Homework Assignment #3 (20 min)**
- Strategy-on-a-Page framework introduction
- Assign sections to participants

**Output:**
- Vision statement for each theme
- Dependency map
- Preliminary sequencing

---

### HOMEWORK PACKAGE #3 (Due 4 days after Session 2)

**Estimated Time:** 1.5 hours
**Format:** Strategy-on-a-Page section drafting
**Assignment:** Each participant drafts 1 section

**Strategy-on-a-Page Sections:**
1. Our Mission & Vision (2030)
2. Strategic Themes (4 themes, 1 sentence each)
3. Key Initiatives (2-3 initiatives per theme)
4. Success Metrics (what we'll measure)
5. 2025-2026 Priorities (what happens first)
6. 2027-2030 Investments (what happens next)

**Instructions:**
- Use provided template
- Keep it concise (bullets, not paragraphs)
- Reference research and homework
- Be specific (avoid buzzwords)

**Deliverable:** Draft section submitted to facilitator

### SESSION 3: Strategy-on-a-Page Development (3 hours, In-Person, Week 3-4)

**Attendees:** All participants
**Pre-Read:** Compiled Strategy-on-a-Page draft (distributed 1 day before)

**Objectives:**
1. Refine and finalize Strategy-on-a-Page
2. Ensure internal consistency and feasibility
3. Build consensus and ownership

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Section Review (90 min)**
- Go through each section
- Challenge assumptions
- Refine language
- Test for clarity: "If someone unfamiliar reads this, will they understand?"

**Part 2: Reality Check (45 min)**
- Budget feasibility
- Timeline realism
- Capability requirements
- Risk mitigation

**Part 3: Visual Design (30 min)**
- Layout and visual hierarchy
- Color-coding priorities
- Infographic elements
- Ensure it fits on ONE page

**Part 4: Homework Assignment #4 (15 min)**
- 90-day action plan assignments

**Output:**
- Near-final Strategy-on-a-Page (needs design polish)
- Agreement on next steps

### HOMEWORK PACKAGE #4 (Due 3 days after Session 3)

**Estimated Time:** 1 hour
**Format:** 90-day action planning
**Assignment:** Each participant identifies actions for their area

**Template:**

| Initiative | Owner | 30 Days | 60 Days | 90 Days | Success Criteria |
|------------|-------|---------|---------|---------|------------------|
|            |       |         |         |         |                  |

**Instructions:**
1. Pick 2-3 initiatives from Strategy-on-a-Page that you'll own/contribute to
2. Break down into 30/60/90-day milestones
3. Identify resource needs and dependencies
4. Define "done" for each milestone

**Deliverable:** Completed action plan table

---

### SESSION 4: Finalization + Accountability (2 hours, Virtual, Week 4)

**Attendees:** All participants

**Objectives:**
1. Finalize Strategy-on-a-Page
2. Lock in 90-day action plan with accountability
3. Establish governance and review cadence

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Final Strategy-on-a-Page Review (30 min)**
- Review designed version
- Final edits and sign-off
- Distribution and communication plan

**Part 2: 90-Day Action Plan Synthesis (60 min)**
- Share individual action plans
- Identify gaps and overlaps
- Assign clear ownership
- Confirm resource allocation

**Part 3: Governance Framework (20 min)**
- Monthly check-ins (30 min, virtual)
- Quarterly deep-dives (half-day, in-person)
- Annual strategy refresh
- Decision-making authority when issues arise

**Part 4: Commitment (10 min)**
- Verbal commitment from each participant
- "What I'm most excited about"
- "What I'm most concerned about"
- "What I commit to doing in the next 30 days"

**Output:**
- Final Strategy-on-a-Page
- 90-day action plan with owners
- Governance calendar for next 12 months

## RISKS AND MITIGATION

### Risk #1: Low Homework Completion
**Mitigation:**
- Kickoff call with personal ask
- Reminder emails at day 3 and day 6
- Make homework USEFUL (not busywork)
- Share completed homework before sessions (peer pressure)

### Risk #2: AMC-Client Misalignment on Priorities
**Mitigation:**
- Surface disagreements in Session 1 (safe space)
- Use data/research to arbitrate disputed facts
- Have decision framework: Client voice = primary (they're the member experts)
- Escalation path if fundamental disagreement

### Risk #3: Too Abstract, Not Actionable
**Mitigation:**
- Force specificity in homework (numbers, dates, examples)
- Reality check in Session 3 (budget, timeline, capability)
- 90-day action plan prevents "strategic shelf-ware"
- Monthly accountability check-ins

### Risk #4: Loss of Momentum Post-Program
**Mitigation:**
- Build governance INTO the program (not after)
- Calendar monthly check-ins before Session 4 ends
- Assign executive sponsor to own follow-through
- 30-day review as "finish line" for first sprint

### Risk #5: Program Expands Beyond 4 Sessions
**Mitigation:**
- Hard stop at 4 sessions (no exceptions)
- Parking lot for "future topics"
- Facilitator enforces time discipline
- If more time needed, it's a DIFFERENT program

## FACILITATION NOTES

### Facilitation Principles
1. **Data Over Opinions**: Reference research constantly
2. **Future Over Past**: No blame, only forward focus
3. **Decisions Over Discussions**: Every session ends with commitments
4. **Clarity Over Consensus**: Better to disagree clearly than agree vaguely

### Handling Difficult Moments

**When AMC and Client Disagree:**
- Reframe as complementary perspectives, not conflict
- Ask: "What would members say?"
- Use research to ground discussion
- Document disagreement and decision rationale

**When Executives Dominate:**
- "Let's hear from someone who hasn't spoken"
- Silent writing time before discussion
- Round-robin sharing (everyone speaks)

**When Discussion Gets Abstract:**
- "Give me an example"
- "What would that look like in 90 days?"
- "How would we measure that?"

**When Energy Drops:**
- Take breaks every 75 minutes
- Use small group discussions
- Change modality (stand up, draw, vote)
