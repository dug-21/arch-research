## ✍️ WRITING PHASE (3-4 hours)

### Step 1: Start with the Headline

**Don't start at the beginning. Start with the headline.**

**Test:** If this was the ONLY thing people read, would they care?

**Formula:**
[Your Organization] + [Achieves What] + [For How Many] + [With What Result]

**Examples:**
- "Association X Launches AI Career Advisor for 50,000 Members, Achieving 300% Engagement Growth"
- "AMC Y Becomes First to Power 100 AI-Augmented Associations, Members Report 10X Value"
- "Client Z Transforms Member Experience, Retention Jumps from 70% to 95% in 3 Years"

**Try 5-7 headlines. Pick the one that makes you most excited/nervous.**

**Estimated Time:** 30 minutes

### Step 2: Write the Problem/Crisis Section

**This is the "why now" section. Make it visceral.**

**Formula:**
- What's broken today?
- What's at stake if we don't fix it?
- What's the trend that makes this urgent?
- What will happen if we do nothing?

**Length:** 2-3 paragraphs

**Example Opening:**
> "For years, professional associations watched helplessly as [specific problem]. Members questioned paying $X annual dues when [alternative] provided [benefit] for free. By [year], [percentage]% of associations had lost [percentage]% of their members, especially [demographic] who saw them as [negative perception] rather than [desired perception]."

**Estimated Time:** 30 minutes

### Step 3: Write the Solution/Transformation Section

**This is "what we built/created/launched."**

**Formula:**
- What did we create? (Specific product/service/platform)
- How does it solve the problem?
- What makes it different/better?
- What can it do?

**Length:** 2-4 paragraphs

**Key:** Be SPECIFIC. Not "AI-powered solutions" but "AI Career Advisor that provides 24/7 personalized guidance based on member's goals and industry trends."

**Estimated Time:** 45 minutes

### Step 4: Write the Customer Impact Section

**This is "what changes for members."**

**Formula:**
- What was their experience before?
- What's their experience now?
- What can they do now they couldn't before?
- What outcomes have they achieved?

**Include METRICS:**
- From [old state] to [new state]
- [Percentage]% improvement in [metric]
- [Number] of members now doing [behavior]

**Estimated Time:** 30 minutes

### Step 5: Create the Customer Quote

**This is your member's voice. Make it REAL.**

**Formula:**
- Name, Title, Organization (make it realistic)
- What they achieved
- How it felt/what changed
- Specific before/after

**DON'T write marketing speak:**
❌ "This revolutionary platform transformed our member engagement!"

**DO write authentic voice:**
✅ "We used to spend 80% of our time on administrative tasks and 20% on member value. Now our AI handles operations while we focus entirely on strategic initiatives. Our members went from checking in quarterly to engaging daily."

**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes

### Step 6: Create the Leadership Quote

**This is YOUR voice (or CEO's). Strategic vision.**

**Formula:**
- What's your key insight about the industry?
- Why did you make this bet?
- What's your vision for the future?
- What does this mean for others?

**Example:**
> "We stopped thinking of ourselves as a back-office service provider and started seeing ourselves as the intelligence layer that makes associations indispensable. When associations can predict what members need before they ask and deliver it instantly, the value proposition becomes irresistible."

**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes

### Step 7: Polish and Refine

**Read your full press release aloud.**

**Tests:**
- [ ] Would a member be excited to read this announcement?
- [ ] Are the metrics specific and credible?
- [ ] Does it sound like news, not marketing?
- [ ] Could you actually deliver this by 2030 if you committed?
- [ ] Is it bold enough to be worth announcing?

**Revise** based on these tests.

**Estimated Time:** 30-45 minutes

## 🤔 FAQ WRITING PHASE (1-2 hours)

### Step 1: Brainstorm Hard Questions

**Ask yourself:**
- What would a skeptical board member ask?
- What would a conservative client ask?
- What would a competitor ask?
- What would an investor ask?
- What are YOU most worried about?

**Write down 15-20 questions.** Don't filter yet.

**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes

### Step 2: Organize into Categories

**Sort your questions into:**
1. Capabilities & Investment
2. Change Management
3. Competitive Dynamics
4. Customer Adoption
5. Risks & Threats
6. Operational Details

**Pick 1-2 hardest questions from each category.**

**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes

### Step 3: Answer Each Question Honestly

**For each FAQ:**
- **Don't sugarcoat.** If you don't know, say "This would require validation of X"
- **Be specific.** Numbers, timelines, resources
- **Show you've thought it through.** Not "We'd hire people" but "We'd need 5 AI engineers ($150K each), 2 data scientists, 1 product manager over 18 months"

**Length:** 1-2 paragraphs per answer

**Estimated Time:** 60-90 minutes

### Step 4: Add One "Existential" Question

**Every FAQ should include:**

"What could make this obsolete?" or "What could make associations irrelevant?"

**Answer honestly.** This shows strategic thinking.

**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes

## 📊 EVALUATION RUBRIC

**Your press release will be evaluated on:**

### 1. Customer-Centricity (25 points)
- [ ] Clearly articulates member value (not just organizational achievement)
- [ ] Customer quote sounds authentic and specific
- [ ] Problem section resonates with real member pain points
- [ ] Measurable customer outcomes included

### 2. Specificity & Concreteness (25 points)
- [ ] Headline is specific and quantified
- [ ] Metrics are included (not vague "improved engagement")
- [ ] Solution describes WHAT you built, not just benefits
- [ ] Timeline and scope are concrete

### 3. Ambition & Vision (20 points)
- [ ] Bold enough to be newsworthy
- [ ] Stretches current capabilities
- [ ] Addresses transformational change, not incremental improvement
- [ ] Inspires excitement

### 4. Credibility & Feasibility (15 points)
- [ ] Realistic by 2030 if fully committed
- [ ] Not science fiction
- [ ] FAQ shows honest thinking about barriers
- [ ] Demonstrates understanding of constraints

### 5. FAQ Quality (15 points)
- [ ] Addresses hard questions, not softballs
- [ ] Honest about challenges and unknowns
- [ ] Shows strategic thinking
- [ ] Covers all required categories

**Total: 100 points**

**High-quality submissions will:**
- Make you excited AND nervous
- Pass the "would we announce this publicly?" test
- Force you to think differently about the future
- Reveal what you don't know (FAQ gaps)

## 💡 TIPS FOR SUCCESS

### DO:
✅ Start with headline - if it's not compelling, rethink the idea
✅ Use specific numbers - "3X engagement" not "significant improvement"
✅ Read it aloud - does it sound like news or marketing?
✅ Think from member's perspective - "What's in it for them?"
✅ Be honest in FAQ - "We don't know X yet" is fine
✅ Reference your research - ground it in reality
✅ Let yourself be bold - this is 2030, not 2026

### DON'T:
❌ Use buzzwords without definition - "AI-powered solutions" means nothing
❌ Make it all about you - members don't care about your process
❌ Write vague "strategic themes" - be concrete
❌ Sugarcoat challenges in FAQ - address them directly
❌ Copy Virtual Inc. exactly - this is YOUR vision
❌ Play it safe - boring press releases mean boring strategies

## ❓ FAQ ABOUT THE HOMEWORK

**Q: Can I collaborate with others?**
A: Talk, brainstorm, share ideas - yes. But everyone submits their own unique press release. We need diverse visions, not groupthink.

**Q: What if my idea seems too ambitious?**
A: Good! The point is to stretch. Ask: "Could this happen by 2030 if we were ALL IN?" If yes, write it.

**Q: What if I have multiple ideas?**
A: Pick ONE for the press release. You can mention alternatives in FAQ or save for Session 2 discussion.

**Q: Should AMC and Client write different press releases?**
A: You can if you want to explore different perspectives. But ideally, the press release represents SHARED success - what you achieve together for members.

**Q: What if I don't know how to answer an FAQ?**
A: Write it anyway and note "This requires research/validation." The FAQ reveals gaps - that's valuable.

**Q: How polished should this be?**
A: Professional quality. You should be comfortable presenting it publicly. But don't obsess over perfection - focus on bold thinking.

**Q: Can I use AI to help write this?**
A: Yes, for research and drafting. But the ideas, vision, and strategic thinking must be YOURS. AI can't write your 2030 vision for you.
