# Session-by-Session Facilitation Guides

**Program:** Strategic Business Engagement (AMC + Client)
**Total Sessions:** 4 (plus kickoff call)
**Timeline:** 3-4 weeks

---

## KICKOFF CALL (30 Minutes, Virtual)

### Objectives
1. Build personal commitment to the program
2. Clarify expectations and deliverables
3. Assign homework with clear deadline
4. Answer questions and concerns

### Pre-Work (Facilitator)
- [ ] Confirm all participants can attend
- [ ] Send calendar invites with Zoom link
- [ ] Prepare slide deck (10 slides max)
- [ ] Have homework ready to distribute

### Agenda

**0:00-0:05 | Opening & Context (5 min)**

*Talking Points:*
- Thank everyone for committing time
- Explain the inflection point: "We're at a critical moment. The next 5 years will determine our competitive position for the decade after."
- Frame urgency: "We need to move fast without sacrificing depth. This program is designed to do both."

**0:05-0:15 | Program Structure Overview (10 min)**

*Show visual timeline:*
```
Kickoff → HW1 → Session 1 → HW2 → Session 2 → HW3 → Session 3 → HW4 → Session 4
Week 0   Week 1   Week 1-2   Week 2   Week 2-3   Week 3   Week 3-4   Week 4
```

*Walk through:*
- 4 sessions total (6 hours in-person, 4.5 hours virtual)
- 4 homework packages (~2 hours each)
- Deliverables: Strategy-on-a-Page + 90-day action plan
- Post-program: Monthly check-ins + quarterly reviews

**0:15-0:25 | Homework Assignment (10 min)**

*Explain Homework #1:*
- Part A: Read 2030 assumptions with verified sources
- Part B: Answer reflection questions (shared + role-specific)
- Part C: Respond to 2 provocative scenarios

*Set expectations:*
- Due in 7 days (specific date)
- Will be shared with all participants 1 day before Session 1
- Candor valued over consensus
- 2-3 hours estimated time

*Emphasize importance:*
- "This isn't busywork. Your homework creates shared context and surfaces issues early. The quality of your preparation determines the quality of our strategy."

**0:25-0:30 | Q&A and Commitment (5 min)**

*Questions to answer:*
- Logistics (dates, times, locations)
- Homework submission process
- Expectations for participation

*Ask for verbal commitment:*
- "Can everyone commit to completing the homework by [date]?"
- "Can everyone commit to attending all 4 sessions?"
- If no, address scheduling conflicts now

### Facilitator Tips
- **Energy matters**: You set the tone. Be enthusiastic but not over-the-top.
- **Senior leader should speak**: Have CEO/President reinforce importance.
- **Address elephants**: If there are known tensions between AMC and client, acknowledge them. "We're here to align, not assign blame."
- **Keep it tight**: 30 minutes max. Respect their time.

### Follow-Up Actions
- [ ] Send recording and slides within 2 hours
- [ ] Send homework package within 24 hours
- [ ] Send reminder email 3 days before homework due
- [ ] Send final reminder 1 day before homework due
- [ ] Compile homework responses and distribute 1 day before Session 1

## SESSION 1: Current State Alignment (3 Hours, In-Person)

### Objectives
1. Build shared understanding of current capabilities and gaps
2. Surface and resolve factual disagreements
3. Identify 4 strategic themes for development

### Pre-Work (Facilitator)
- [ ] All homework received and compiled
- [ ] Create synthesis document with themes
- [ ] Prepare whiteboard/collaboration space
- [ ] Print homework summaries for reference
- [ ] Arrange room with small group tables

### Materials Needed
- Compiled homework responses (printed)
- Research documents from previous work
- Whiteboard + markers
- Sticky notes + sharpies
- Dot voting stickers
- Laptop + projector

### Detailed Agenda

**0:00-0:10 | Opening & Ground Rules (10 min)**

*Welcome + Energy Check:*
- Thank everyone for homework completion
- Quick round: "In one word, how are you feeling about this program?"

*Ground Rules Reminder:*
1. Candor over consensus
2. Data over opinion
3. Future over past
4. Respect dissent
5. Stay on time

*Agenda Overview:*
Walk through the 3-hour plan visually

**0:10-1:10 | Homework Share-Out (60 min)**

*Structure:*
- Break into 2 mixed groups (AMC + Client in each)
- Each group: 20 min discussion, 10 min synthesis
- Full group: 20 min share-back

*Small Group Instructions:*
"Discuss your homework responses. Focus on:
1. What surprised you in others' answers?
2. Where do you see alignment?
3. Where do you see disagreement?
4. What themes are emerging?"

*Facilitator Role:*
- Circulate between groups
- Listen for interesting insights
- Note patterns and outliers
- Prepare to synthesize

*Share-Back:*
- Each group presents 3-5 key insights
- Capture on whiteboard under:
  - Inevitable trends
  - Uncertain trends
  - Current gaps
  - Aspirations

**1:10-2:10 | Current State Assessment (60 min)**

*Activity: Stop/Start/Continue + What's Missing*

*Instructions:*
"We're going to assess our current state honestly. Use sticky notes to capture:"

**What's Working (Keep Doing):**
- Technology, processes, relationships that deliver value
- Examples: "Client responsiveness," "Event platform reliability"

**What's Not Working (Stop or Fix):**
- Pain points for AMC, client, or members
- Examples: "Clunky member portal," "Manual reporting processes"

**What's Missing (Start Doing):**
- Gaps in capabilities, offerings, processes
- Examples: "AI-powered recommendations," "Mobile app," "Predictive analytics"

*Process:*
1. Silent brainstorm (10 min) - everyone writes sticky notes
2. Post on walls under each category (10 min)
3. Group similar items (15 min)
4. Discuss top 3-5 in each category (25 min)

*Reality Check:*
Pull in data from previous research to validate/challenge perceptions
- "The research shows 60% of members want X. Are we seeing that?"
- "Industry benchmark for Y is Z. How do we compare?"

**2:10-2:25 | BREAK (15 min)**

**2:25-3:25 | Strategic Themes Identification (60 min)**

*Transition:*
"Based on homework + current state, we need to identify 4 strategic themes that will organize our strategy for 2025-2030."

*Brainstorm Themes (20 min):*
- What patterns did you see in homework?
- What gaps are most critical to address?
- What opportunities are biggest?
- What threats require response?

*Example themes to prompt thinking:*
- AI-Powered Member Experience
- Revenue Diversification & Growth
- Operational Excellence & Automation
- Data-Driven Decision Making
- Mobile-First Digital Transformation
- Security & Trust as Differentiator
- Generational Engagement Shift
- Strategic Partnerships & Ecosystem

*Refine themes (20 min):*
- Group similar ideas
- Write theme statements: "[Theme Name]: [One-sentence description]"
- Ensure themes are:
  - Distinct (not overlapping)
  - Measurable (can track progress)
  - Actionable (can invest in)
  - Comprehensive (cover major strategic needs)

*Prioritize themes (20 min):*
- Aim for 4 themes (not more, not fewer)
- Use dot voting: Everyone gets 8 dots, distribute across themes
- Discuss top 4-5, refine to final 4
- Confirm: "Are these the 4 themes we want to organize our strategy around?"

**3:25-3:35 | Homework #2 Assignment (10 min)**

*Explain Theme Deep-Dive Assignment:*
- Each participant chooses 1-2 themes to research
- Answer 6 questions (provided in homework doc)
- Find 2-3 external examples/case studies
- Prepare 5-min presentation for Session 2

*Assign themes:*
- Ensure each theme has 2-3 people researching
- Mix AMC + Client on each theme
- Provide research materials and question template

**3:35-3:45 | Close & Commitments (10 min)**

*Recap:*
- We aligned on current state
- We identified 4 strategic themes
- We have homework for theme deep-dives

*Check energy:*
- "How are we feeling? What went well? What could be better?"

*Commitments:*
- Homework due in 4 days
- Session 2 in [X] days (virtual)
- "See you then!"

### Facilitator Tips

**If discussion goes off track:**
- "That's important—let's put it in the parking lot"
- "How does this relate to [current topic]?"
- "We have 10 minutes left on this—what's most critical?"

**If AMC and Client disagree:**
- "Both perspectives are valuable. Let's capture both."
- "What data would help us resolve this?"
- "Can this disagreement inform our strategy?"

**If energy drops:**
- Call break early
- Change modality (stand up, move around)
- Use humor or personal stories
- Refocus on "why this matters"

### Post-Session Actions
- [ ] Document session notes (capture board photos)
- [ ] Synthesize strategic themes into 1-pager
- [ ] Distribute homework #2 within 24 hours
- [ ] Send session summary with action items
- [ ] Individual outreach to anyone who seemed disengaged

## SESSION 2: 2030 Vision + Theme Development (2 Hours, Virtual)

### Objectives
1. Develop detailed 2030 vision for each strategic theme
2. Identify interdependencies between themes
3. Begin prioritization and sequencing

### Pre-Work (Facilitator)
- [ ] Compile theme research slides from homework
- [ ] Create shared collaboration board (Miro/Mural)
- [ ] Prepare session deck with template frameworks
- [ ] Test technology and virtual setup

### Materials Needed
- Compiled theme research presentations
- Virtual whiteboard (Miro, Mural, or Jamboard)
- Session deck with frameworks
- Shared note-taking doc

### Detailed Agenda

**0:00-0:05 | Opening & Session Overview (5 min)**

*Welcome:*
- Energy check: "Quick poll: How's everyone doing?"
- Remind: Cameras on, mute when not speaking
- Agenda walkthrough

**0:05-1:05 | Theme Presentations + Discussion (60 min)**

*For Each Theme (4 themes × 15 min = 60 min):*

**Presentation (5 min):**
- 1-2 presenters share research
- Use slide template:
  - Vision statement (what great looks like in 2030)
  - Current gap (where we are vs. where we need to be)
  - Quick wins (90-day opportunities)
  - Big bets (1-2 year investments)
  - Success metrics (how we'll measure)
  - Risks (what could prevent success)

**Discussion (10 min):**
Facilitator asks:
1. "What resonates? What concerns you?"
2. "Is the vision ambitious enough? Too ambitious?"
3. "Are the metrics measurable? Meaningful?"
4. "What's missing?"

*Capture refinements on virtual whiteboard*

**0:05-1:05 | Theme Presentations (continued)**

*Facilitator Role:*
- Keep time rigorously (use timer)
- Encourage dissent: "Who disagrees? Why?"
- Push for specificity: "What does that look like concretely?"
- Connect to research: "The data shows... how does that inform this?"

**1:05-1:15 | BREAK (10 min)**

**1:15-1:55 | Integration Discussion (40 min)**

*Part 1: Dependencies (15 min)*

"Let's map how these themes connect."

*Activity:*
- Draw themes on virtual whiteboard
- Draw arrows: What enables what?
- Example: "AI-Powered Experience" needs "Data Infrastructure" + "Security"

*Questions:*
- Which themes are foundational (must do first)?
- Which themes are dependent (need others to succeed)?
- Are there conflicts? (e.g., "Move fast" vs. "Build security")

*Part 2: Sequencing (15 min)*

"We can't do everything at once. What's the logical order?"

*Activity:*
- Group themes into phases:
  - Phase 1 (2025-2026): Foundation
  - Phase 2 (2027-2028): Scale
  - Phase 3 (2029-2030): Transform

- For each theme, identify:
  - What starts in Phase 1?
  - What ramps in Phase 2?
  - What matures in Phase 3?

*Part 3: Trade-Offs (10 min)*

"We can't do everything. What are we choosing NOT to do?"

*Questions:*
- If budget is constrained, what's the priority order?
- If we had to cut one theme, which and why?
- Where might we partner vs. build?

**1:55-2:10 | Homework #3 Assignment & Close (15 min)**

*Explain Strategy-on-a-Page Assignment:*
- Introduce template and framework
- Assign sections to participants
  - Mission/Vision: [Names]
  - Theme 1: [Names]
  - Theme 2: [Names]
  - Theme 3: [Names]
  - Theme 4: [Names]
  - Roadmap: [Names]
  - Metrics: [Names]

*Instructions:*
- Use template provided
- Be concise (bullets, not paragraphs)
- Reference homework and session discussions
- Due in 4 days

*Close:*
- "Great progress today. We have vision + themes + sequencing."
- "Next session we'll turn this into a Strategy-on-a-Page."
- "See you in [X] days for Session 3 (in-person)."

### Facilitator Tips

**For virtual facilitation:**
- Use breakout rooms if group >10 people
- Leverage polls and chat for engagement
- Share screen for presentations, virtual whiteboard for collaboration
- Take breaks every 60-75 minutes

**If discussions lag:**
- Call on specific people: "[Name], what's your take?"
- Use chat: "Everyone type one word describing this theme"
- Show example: "Here's how another organization approached this"

### Post-Session Actions
- [ ] Document session notes and decisions
- [ ] Refine theme vision statements based on discussion
- [ ] Create dependency map visual
- [ ] Distribute homework #3 (Strategy-on-a-Page sections)
- [ ] Send session recording and chat transcript

## SESSION 3: Strategy-on-a-Page Development (3 Hours, In-Person)

### Objectives
1. Refine and finalize Strategy-on-a-Page
2. Ensure internal consistency and feasibility
3. Build consensus and ownership

### Pre-Work (Facilitator)
- [ ] Compile all homework sections into draft Strategy-on-a-Page
- [ ] Identify gaps, inconsistencies, or conflicts
- [ ] Prepare questions for each section
- [ ] Create visual draft (even if rough)

### Materials Needed
- Printed draft Strategy-on-a-Page (one per person)
- Large poster version for wall review
- Markers, sticky notes for edits
- Laptop + projector for live editing
- Research documents for reference

### Detailed Agenda

**0:00-0:10 | Opening & Draft Review (10 min)**

*Welcome:*
- "Today we turn our work into a final strategy document."
- "This will be a living document we use to guide decisions."

*Draft Overview:*
- Walk through compiled draft
- Acknowledge contributors
- Set expectations: "We'll refine, debate, and finalize today."

**0:10-1:40 | Section-by-Section Review (90 min)**

*For Each Section (~12-15 min per section):*

**Read Aloud:**
- Section owner reads current draft

**Questions:**
1. "Does this accurately capture our intent?"
2. "Is it specific enough? Too specific?"
3. "Will someone unfamiliar understand this?"
4. "What's missing?"
5. "What should be cut?"

**Refine:**
- Live editing on projected version
- Capture changes
- Move on when section reaches "good enough" (not perfect)

*Sections to Review:*
1. Mission & Vision
2. Strategic Theme 1
3. Strategic Theme 2
4. Strategic Theme 3
5. Strategic Theme 4
6. Metrics Dashboard
7. Execution Roadmap

*Facilitator Role:*
- Keep time strictly
- Push for clarity: "What does that mean in practice?"
- Challenge jargon: "Can we say this in plain language?"
- Test understanding: "Give me an example of what this looks like."

**1:40-1:55 | BREAK (15 min)**

**1:55-2:40 | Reality Check & Risk Assessment (45 min)**

*Budget Feasibility (15 min):*
- "Let's talk numbers. What will this cost?"
- Review investment framework
- Sanity check: Can we afford this?
- Adjust priorities if needed

*Timeline Realism (15 min):*
- "Are these timelines achievable?"
- Do we have the people, skills, capacity?
- Where might we be over-optimistic?
- What dependencies could delay us?

*Risk Mitigation (15 min):*
- "What could derail this strategy?"
- Identify top 5 risks
- For each: likelihood, impact, mitigation
- Assign risk owners

**2:40-3:10 | Visual Design & One-Page Test (30 min)**

*Layout Review:*
- Look at visual draft on poster
- Does it actually fit on ONE page?
- Is visual hierarchy clear?
- Can you scan it in 60 seconds and grasp the strategy?

*Design Decisions:*
- Color-coding for themes?
- Icons or graphics?
- Font sizes and emphasis
- White space and breathing room

*Iteration:*
- Make adjustments to layout
- Prioritize what's most important
- Cut anything that's "nice to have but not essential"

**3:10-3:25 | Homework #4 Assignment (15 min)**

*Explain 90-Day Action Plan:*
- Each person identifies 2-3 initiatives they own or contribute to
- Break into 30/60/90-day milestones
- Define success criteria
- Identify resource needs

*Template Walkthrough:*
Show table structure and examples

*Due Date:*
3 days before Session 4

**3:25-3:35 | Commitments & Close (10 min)**

*Recap:*
- We have a near-final Strategy-on-a-Page
- It will be designed and polished for Session 4
- We have 90-day action plan assignment

*Verbal Commitment:*
- "Can everyone commit to living this strategy?"
- "What concerns remain?"
- "What are you most excited about?"

*Preview Session 4:*
- Final review + sign-off
- 90-day action plan
- Governance framework
- Launch plan

### Facilitator Tips

**If people get stuck on perfection:**
- "Good enough is good enough. We can refine in quarterly reviews."
- "Done is better than perfect."
- "This is version 1.0, not the final version ever."

**If disagreements emerge:**
- "Let's capture both perspectives and decide."
- "What data would help us choose?"
- "Can we pilot both and learn?"

**If energy is low:**
- "Stand up, stretch, walk around for 2 minutes."
- "Let's shift from critique to excitement—what's working?"
- Remember why: "This strategy shapes the next 5 years."

### Post-Session Actions
- [ ] Incorporate all edits into master document
- [ ] Design professional visual version
- [ ] Distribute homework #4 (90-day action plan)
- [ ] Send updated Strategy-on-a-Page draft for review
- [ ] Prepare final version for Session 4 sign-off

## SESSION 4: Finalization + Accountability (2 Hours, Virtual)

### Objectives
1. Finalize Strategy-on-a-Page with sign-off
2. Lock in 90-day action plan with clear accountability
3. Establish governance and review cadence
4. Celebrate and commit

### Pre-Work (Facilitator)
- [ ] Final designed Strategy-on-a-Page ready
- [ ] Compile 90-day action plans from homework
- [ ] Create accountability tracker template
- [ ] Prepare governance calendar
- [ ] Plan celebration moment

### Materials Needed
- Final Strategy-on-a-Page (PDF, shareable)
- Compiled 90-day action plan
- Accountability tracker
- Governance calendar template
- Virtual celebration (e.g., toast, shoutouts)

### Detailed Agenda

**0:00-0:05 | Opening & Energy (5 min)**

*Welcome:*
- "This is our final session. We're going to finalize, commit, and celebrate."

*Quick Round:*
- "One word: How are you feeling about this process?"

**0:05-0:35 | Final Strategy-on-a-Page Review (30 min)**

*Presentation:*
- Share screen with designed Strategy-on-a-Page
- Walk through each section
- Highlight changes from Session 3

*Final Edits:*
- "Any final concerns or changes?"
- Make minor edits (not major revisions)
- "Does everyone see themselves in this strategy?"

*Sign-Off:*
- "Can we approve this as our official strategy for 2025-2030?"
- Verbal agreement from each participant
- Documented for record

**0:35-1:35 | 90-Day Action Plan Synthesis (60 min)**

*Share Individual Plans (30 min):*
- Each person presents their 2-3 initiatives
- 30/60/90-day milestones
- Resource needs
- Dependencies

*Facilitator captures on shared tracker*

*Identify Gaps & Overlaps (15 min):*
- "What's missing? What initiatives have no owner?"
- "Where do we have duplication?"
- Adjust and fill gaps

*Confirm Ownership (15 min):*
- "For each initiative, who's the owner?"
- "Who's accountable for delivery?"
- "What support do you need?"

*Create Master 90-Day Plan with clear accountability*

**1:35-1:45 | BREAK (10 min)**

**1:45-2:05 | Governance Framework (20 min)**

*Monthly Check-Ins:*
- 30 minutes, virtual
- Review 30-day milestones
- Surface blockers
- Adjust as needed

*Quarterly Deep-Dives:*
- Half-day, in-person
- Review metrics
- Assess strategic themes
- Make course corrections

*Annual Strategy Refresh:*
- Full-day session
- Update assumptions
- Revise priorities
- Celebrate wins

*Schedule Right Now:*
- Calendar next 3 monthly check-ins
- Calendar next 2 quarterly reviews
- Assign facilitators/owners

*Decision-Making Authority:*
- Clarify who decides what
- Escalation path for conflicts
- Expectation setting

**2:05-2:15 | Communication Plan (10 min)**

*Internal:*
- How do we roll this out to broader teams?
- All-hands presentation? Written memo? Workshop?
- Timeline for communication

*External:*
- What do we share with board, stakeholders, members?
- Communication materials needed?

*Assign Owners for Communication Plan*

**2:15-2:30 | Commitment Circle & Close (15 min)**

*Commitment Round:*
Each person shares:
1. "What I'm most excited about from this strategy"
2. "What I'm most concerned about"
3. "What I personally commit to doing in the next 30 days"

*Celebration:*
- Acknowledge the work done
- Thank facilitator and participants
- Virtual toast or group shoutout
- "We did it!"

*Final Words:*
- "This strategy is not what we wrote—it's what we do."
- "See you at the first monthly check-in in 30 days."
- "Let's make this happen!"

### Facilitator Tips

**For commitment round:**
- Go in order, everyone speaks
- No interrupting
- Capture commitments for accountability

**If resistance emerges:**
- "What would need to change for you to feel comfortable?"
- "What's the minimum viable version you could support?"
- "Can we pilot and learn?"

**Celebrate the process:**
- This was intense work
- Acknowledge progress
- Build momentum for execution

### Post-Session Actions
- [ ] Distribute final Strategy-on-a-Page (PDF + editable)
- [ ] Send master 90-day action plan with owners
- [ ] Send governance calendar with invites
- [ ] Create communication materials
- [ ] Schedule 30-day check-in
- [ ] Send thank-you note to all participants
- [ ] Archive all session materials for future reference

## POST-PROGRAM: Monthly Check-Ins (30 Minutes, Virtual)

### Purpose
Maintain momentum, track 30-day milestones, surface blockers

### Agenda (30 min)
1. **Progress Review (20 min):**
   - Each initiative owner: 2-min update
   - What's done? What's in progress? What's blocked?

2. **Blocker Resolution (8 min):**
   - What needs help?
   - Who can unblock?
   - Decisions needed?

3. **Next 30 Days (2 min):**
   - Confirm next milestones
   - Any adjustments needed?

### Keep It Tight
- Start and end on time
- Use tracker document
- No long discussions (escalate to offline)

---

## POST-PROGRAM: Quarterly Deep-Dives (Half-Day, In-Person)

### Purpose
Strategic review, metrics assessment, course correction

### Agenda (3-4 hours)
1. **Metrics Review (45 min):**
   - Dashboard walkthrough
   - Progress vs. targets
   - Trends and insights

2. **Theme Assessment (60 min):**
   - Each theme: What's working? What's not?
   - Adjust priorities if needed

3. **Strategic Discussion (60 min):**
   - Market changes? New opportunities? Emerging risks?
   - Assumption validation
   - Pivot decisions

4. **Action Plan Refresh (30 min):**
   - Update next 90 days
   - Resource reallocation
   - Commitment renewal

*This completes the session-by-session facilitation guides. Each session is designed to build on the previous, maintain momentum, and produce tangible outcomes.*
