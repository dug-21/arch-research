# SESSION 2: Press Release Presentations & Strategic Debate

**Duration:** 3 hours, In-Person
**Participants:** AMC Leadership + Client Leadership
**Pre-Read:** All submitted press releases (distributed 1 day before)

## 🗓️ DETAILED AGENDA

### PART 1: Opening & Frame (15 min) | 0:00-0:15

**Welcome & Energy Check:**
> "Welcome to Session 2. You've all written the future. Today we decide which future to pursue."

**Acknowledge Effort:**
- Thank everyone for homework completion
- Recognize the difficulty of the assignment
- Set expectations: "Today will be inspiring AND uncomfortable"

**Review Synthesis:**
- Share the themes document
- Point out patterns and outliers
- "Here's what I noticed across your visions..."

**Ground Rules for Today:**

1. **Presenters:** You have 5 minutes. Practice your elevator pitch.
2. **Audience:** Listen first, critique later. Take notes on what excites you.
3. **Debate:** We're debating IDEAS, not people. Pushback is love.
4. **Decision:** By end of session, we choose 1-2 directions to pursue.
5. **Candor:** If you think something won't work, say so (with reasons).

**Presentation Order:**
- Announced upfront (strategic sequencing - see below)

---

### PART 2: Press Release Presentations (90 min) | 0:15-1:45

**Structure: 10-12 minutes per person (if 8 participants)**
- 5 min: Present press release
- 5-7 min: Q&A and discussion

#### Presentation Instructions

**For Presenters:**

> "You have 5 minutes to sell us on your 2030 vision. We've all read your press release, so don't just read it verbatim. Instead, tell us:
>
> 1. **The Headline** - What are we announcing?
> 2. **Why This Matters** - What problem does this solve for members?
> 3. **The Big Bet** - What's the boldest part of this vision?
> 4. **The Hard Part** - What's the biggest challenge in your FAQ?
> 5. **The Ask** - What would make you choose this direction?"

**Timing:** Use visible timer. Give 30-second warning.

#### Discussion Protocol

**After each presentation, facilitator leads 5-7 min discussion:**

**Round 1: Appreciations (2 min)**
- "What excites you about this vision?"
- "What's the strongest part of this press release?"
- Capture on whiteboard

**Round 2: Questions/Clarifications (2 min)**
- "What needs more detail?"
- "How would X actually work?"
- Presenter answers briefly

**Round 3: Challenges (2-3 min)**
- "What concerns you about this?"
- "What could make this fail?"
- "What's missing?"
- Presenter acknowledges, doesn't defend

**Capture:** Scribe takes notes on:
- Key strengths
- Key concerns
- Unique ideas worth preserving

---

### PRESENTATION SEQUENCING STRATEGY

**DON'T go alphabetically or volunteer-based. Sequence strategically:**

**Opening:** Start with STRONGEST presentation
- Sets high bar
- Energizes the room
- Creates "beat this" dynamic

**Middle:** Alternate between similar and different visions
- Group similar ideas to show patterns
- Intersperse outliers to maintain interest
- Build momentum

**Closing:** End with MOST PROVOCATIVE or OUTLIER
- Ends on bold note
- Prevents consensus-too-soon
- Creates "wait, what if?" energy

**Use break to:**

### PART 3: Comparative Discussion (30 min) | 2:00-2:30

**Facilitator leads synthesis discussion:**

**Activity 1: What Did You Notice? (10 min)**

Ask:
- "What themes appeared across multiple visions?"
- "Which ideas were unique?"
- "Where did you see contradictions or tensions?"

Capture on whiteboard:
```
CONVERGENT IDEAS (appeared in multiple press releases):
- [Theme 1]
- [Theme 2]
- [Theme 3]

DIVERGENT IDEAS (unique visions):
- [Outlier 1]
- [Outlier 2]

TENSIONS/TRADE-OFFS:
- Build platform vs. Partner with existing
- Aggressive timeline vs. Phased approach
- Revenue-driven vs. Impact-driven metrics
```

**Activity 2: The Tough Questions (20 min)**

**For each convergent theme, ask:**

1. **Customer Test:** "Would members actually pay for this?"
2. **Capability Test:** "Can we realistically build this by 2030?"
3. **Competitive Test:** "What prevents someone else from doing this first/better?"
4. **Resource Test:** "What would this cost and how do we fund it?"

**For outlier ideas, ask:**

1. "Is this brilliant or crazy?"
2. "What would have to be true for this to work?"
3. "Should we integrate elements into mainstream vision?"

---

### PART 4: Direction Selection (45 min) | 2:30-3:15

**Goal:** Choose 1-2 strategic directions to develop further

#### Option A: Single Direction (Recommended)

**If convergence is strong, choose ONE press release/direction:**

**Process:**
1. **Dot Voting (10 min)**
   - Everyone gets 3 dots
   - Vote for press releases that:
     - Excite you most
     - Seem most achievable
     - Would differentiate us most
   - Can put multiple dots on same vision

2. **Discussion of Results (15 min)**
   - What's the leading vision?
   - What concerns remain?
   - What elements from other visions should we integrate?

3. **Refinement Commitments (15 min)**
   - How do we strengthen the chosen direction?
   - What from other press releases should we incorporate?
   - Who will own refining this vision?

4. **Decision (5 min)**
   - Formal adoption: "This is our 2030 vision"
   - Document decision and rationale

#### Option B: Dual Track (If Two Strong, Different Directions Emerge)

**If there are two compelling but different visions:**

**Process:**
1. **Identify the Two Paths (10 min)**
   - Name them clearly
   - Articulate how they differ
   - Understand trade-offs

2. **Scenario Planning (20 min)**
   - What would success look like for Path A?
   - What would success look like for Path B?
   - Can we pilot both? Or must we choose?
   - What data would help us decide?

3. **Decision Framework (15 min)**
   - Choose now based on best judgment, OR
   - Define what information we need to decide
   - Set decision deadline (don't let this linger)

**NOTE:** Dual track is HARDER and risks lack of focus. Only pursue if both directions are truly compelling and can be resourced.

---

### PART 5: FAQ Assignment & Close (15 min) | 3:15-3:30

**Now that we have a direction, assign FAQ development:**

**Homework #2: Collaborative FAQ for Chosen Direction**

**Instructions:**
> "We've chosen our 2030 vision. Now we need to answer the HARD questions together. I'm assigning each of you 2-3 FAQ questions to research and answer in depth."

**FAQ Categories & Assignments:**

1. **Capabilities & Technology Build**
   - Assigned to: [Technical leaders]
   - Questions: What tech must we build vs. buy? What's the architecture? What talent do we need?

2. **Financial Model & Investment**
   - Assigned to: [CFO, finance leaders]
   - Questions: What does this cost? How do we fund it? What's the ROI model? What's the pricing strategy?

3. **Change Management & Stakeholder Alignment**
   - Assigned to: [HR, operations leaders]
   - Questions: How do we bring boards along? What about staff concerns? What's the change management plan?

4. **Competitive Positioning & Moats**
   - Assigned to: [Strategy, business development leaders]
   - Questions: What's our defensible advantage? How do we respond to competitors? What makes us hard to replicate?

5. **Customer Adoption & Proof Points**
   - Assigned to: [Client-facing leaders]
   - Questions: Why would members choose this? How do we prove value? What's the adoption timeline?

6. **Risk Mitigation & Failure Modes**
   - Assigned to: [Risk, legal, senior leaders]
   - Questions: What could go wrong? What could make this obsolete? What are contingency plans?

**Deliverable:**
- Each person answers 2-3 FAQs in depth
- 1-2 pages per person
- Research-backed, specific answers
- Due: [1 week before Session 3]

**Close:**
- Recap decision made today
- Acknowledge difficulty of choosing
- Set expectations for Session 3 (Strategy-on-a-Page development)
- Thank everyone for bold thinking

## 🎨 FACILITATION TECHNIQUES

### Managing Presentations

**If someone goes over time:**
- 30-second warning (hold up hand signal)
- At 5 minutes: "Thank you, let's move to questions"
- If they keep going: "We need to respect everyone's time. Final sentence please."

**If presentation is confusing:**
- Stop and clarify: "Before we continue, can you restate the headline?"
- Ask for specifics: "Give us a concrete example of what you mean"

**If presentation is timid:**
- Encourage: "What's the boldest part of this? Sell us on it."
- Push: "If you had to pick ONE metric to brag about, what would it be?"

### Managing Discussion

**If discussion gets too polite/consensus-seeking:**
- "Who disagrees? We need dissent."
- "What's the biggest flaw in this idea?"
- "If you were our competitor, how would you beat this?"

**If discussion gets personal/defensive:**
- "We're critiquing the idea, not the person."
- "What data would help us resolve this?"
- Redirect to customer: "What would members say?"

**If discussion goes off track:**
- "That's important - let's parking lot it and return to [topic]"
- "We have 3 minutes left on this presentation. What's most critical?"

**If energy drops:**
- Call break early
- Stand up, move around
- Change modality: "Everyone write your top concern on a sticky note"

### Driving to Decision

**If group is stuck/can't choose:**
- "What data would help us decide?"
- "Let's pilot for 90 days and reassess"
- "What's the cost of NOT deciding today?"

**If there's one holdout:**
- "Help us understand your concern"
- "What would need to be true for you to support this?"
- "Can you live with this decision even if not your first choice?"

**If decision feels forced/premature:**
- "Are we ready to commit? Or do we need more information?"
- "What's the risk of deciding today vs. waiting?"
- It's OK to say "We need 1 more week to research X" - but set clear deadline

- Read all press releases multiple times

- Encourage bold thinking and dissent

- Capture all good ideas (even from non-chosen paths)

**After Session:**
- Document decision immediately while fresh
- Send detailed notes within 24 hours
- Personal outreach to anyone who seemed disengaged
- Confirm FAQ homework assignments
- Prep for Session 3 (Strategy-on-a-Page development)
