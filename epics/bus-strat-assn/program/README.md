## 📋 Program Overview

### Timeline
| Week | Activity | Duration | Output |
|------|----------|----------|--------|
| Week 0 | Kickoff Call | 30 min (virtual) | Homework assigned |

**Total Time Investment:** ~15-18 hours per participant over 3-4 weeks

1. **Strategy-on-a-Page** - Visual, sharable 1-page strategic plan for 2025-2030
2. **90-Day Action Plan** - Specific initiatives with owners, milestones, and success criteria
3. **Governance Calendar** - Monthly check-ins + quarterly reviews for next 12 months
4. **Communication Materials** - Decks/docs for sharing with broader teams

## 💡 Key Design Principles

### Why This Structure?

**1. Shared Homework = Shared Language**
- AMC and client read same trends and assumptions
- Creates common vocabulary and context
- Surfaces disagreements early and privately

**2. Mixed In-Person + Virtual**
- In-person for hard conversations (trust-building)
- Virtual for refinement and updates (efficiency)
- Total in-person time: 6 hours (respectful of travel)

**3. Four Sessions, No More**
- Scope control prevents endless discussions
- Forces prioritization and decision-making
- Respects busy executive schedules

**4. Strategy-on-a-Page**
- If it doesn't fit on one page, it's not clear enough
- Visual = memorable and sharable
- Living document (not shelf-ware)

**5. 90-Day Action Plan**
- Immediate momentum prevents "strategic drift"
- Clear owners = accountability
- 30-day milestones = fast feedback loops

**6. Built-In Governance**
- Monthly check-ins prevent backsliding
- Quarterly reviews allow course corrections
- Annual refresh keeps strategy current

## ⚠️ Critical Feedback on Original Approach

*(Extracted from `program-overview.md`)*

### What Was Proposed
1. Send separate homework to AMC and client
2. Homework then initiate program
3. Undefined number of sessions
4. Strategy-on-a-Page approach (good!)

### What Was Wrong
1. **Separate homework creates divergence** before first session
   - Better: ONE shared packet with role-specific questions at end

2. **No kickoff call = low homework completion**
   - Better: 30-min kickoff to build commitment

3. **Undefined scope = calendar chaos**
   - Better: Exactly 4 sessions, no more

4. **Missing current state alignment**
   - Better: Session 1 focuses on "where we are" before "where we're going"

### What Was Missing
- Conflict resolution plan (AMC vs. client priorities)
- Decision authority framework
- "What good looks like" definition
- Follow-through mechanism (governance)

**All of these gaps are addressed in this program design.**

## 🔧 Customization Guide

### For Different Organization Sizes

**Small (<20 people):**
- Reduce to 6-8 participants
- Shorten sessions by 20-30%
- Simplify Strategy-on-a-Page (3 themes instead of 4)

**Large (>100 people):**
- Keep core group at 8-12 participants
- Add "stakeholder review" sessions for input
- Create multiple 90-day plans for different divisions

### For Different Industries

**Tech Companies:**
- Emphasize AI, product development, talent competition
- Focus on speed and innovation

**Professional Associations:**
- Emphasize member experience, generational shift, revenue diversification
- Focus on community and value proposition

**Healthcare/Education:**
- Emphasize compliance, equity, impact measurement
- Focus on mission and stakeholder alignment

### For Different Timelines

**Compressed (2 weeks):**
- Reduce homework time (1 hour each)
- Make Sessions 1 & 3 virtual (2 hours each)
- Higher risk of lower quality

**Extended (6 weeks):**
- Add Session 5 for "dry run" of strategy rollout
- More time for research and reflection
- Risk of losing momentum

## 🚨 Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Low Homework Completion
**Symptoms:** <80% complete homework
**Solutions:**
- Personal outreach from senior leader
- Extend deadline by 2-3 days
- Offer 1-on-1 help sessions
- Reduce homework scope for next round

### AMC-Client Fundamental Disagreement
**Symptoms:** Can't agree on priorities or vision
**Solutions:**
- Surface disagreement explicitly in Session 1
- Use research/data to arbitrate facts
- Client voice = primary for member-facing decisions
- AMC voice = primary for capability decisions
- Escalate to senior leadership if needed

### Sessions Going Over Time
**Symptoms:** Can't finish agenda in allotted time
**Solutions:**
- Tighten facilitation (use timers)
- Move less critical items to "parking lot"
- Extend next session by 30-60 min
- Simplify deliverables (3 themes instead of 4)

### Loss of Momentum Post-Program
**Symptoms:** Monthly check-ins skipped, action plan stalled
**Solutions:**
- Senior leader personally schedules next check-in
- Share 90-day plan progress publicly
- Celebrate early wins
- Tie action plan to performance reviews/bonuses
