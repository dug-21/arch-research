## 🎯 HONEST ASSESSMENT OF YOUR PROPOSED APPROACH

### ✅ What You Got RIGHT

1. **Homework with Thought-Provoking Questions**
   - EXCELLENT idea
   - Creates psychological commitment
   - Surfaces disagreements early
   - Pre-work ensures productive sessions

2. **2030 Assumptions Framework**
   - SMART approach
   - Forces concrete thinking vs. vague "vision"
   - Grounds discussion in verifiable trends
   - Separates facts from opinions

3. **Strategy-on-a-Page Concept**
   - PERFECT for executives
   - Visual = memorable and sharable
   - Prevents "strategic shelf-ware"
   - Forces clarity and prioritization

4. **Sense of Urgency**
   - CRITICAL for this audience
   - Prevents endless discussion cycles
   - Drives decision-making
   - Respects busy schedules

### ⚠️ What Needs ADJUSTMENT

1. **Two Separate Homework Packets is WRONG**
   - **Problem:** Creates divergent mental models BEFORE first session
   - **Risk:** AMC and client arrive misaligned, have to backtrack
   - **Fix:** ONE shared packet with role-specific questions at the end
   - **Why:** Shared context = shared language = productive discussion

2. **"Send Homework Then Initiate" is BACKWARDS**
   - **Problem:** People won't do homework without understanding WHY
   - **Fix:** 30-min kickoff call → homework → Session 1
   - **Why:** Personal commitment dramatically improves completion rates

3. **Undefined Number of Sessions is ANXIETY-INDUCING**
   - **Problem:** "Can't be a lot" is vague and scary for calendars
   - **Fix:** Exactly 4 sessions, defined upfront, no exceptions
   - **Why:** Clear scope prevents scope creep and enables planning

4. **Missing Current State Alignment**
   - **Problem:** You start with "future vision" but lack clarity on TODAY
   - **Fix:** Session 1 establishes shared understanding of current state
   - **Why:** Can't agree on direction if you disagree on location

### 🚨 What's MISSING (Critical Gaps)

1. **No Conflict Resolution Plan**
   - AMC and client WILL have different priorities
   - Need explicit process for disagreement
   - Without it: polite discussions, no decisions

2. **No Decision Authority Framework**
   - Who decides when AMC and client disagree?
   - What if homework reveals fundamental misalignment?
   - Clarity prevents frustration and deadlock

3. **No "What Good Looks Like" Definition**
   - How will you know this succeeded?
   - Need concrete outcomes, not "better alignment"
   - Measurable success criteria drive focus

4. **No Follow-Through Mechanism**
   - Strategy without execution = expensive decoration
   - Need 30/60/90-day accountability built IN
   - Governance calendar must be part of program, not after

## 💡 THE BETTER APPROACH (What I've Built)

### Program Structure: 4 Sessions Over 3-4 Weeks

| Session | Duration | Format | Output |
|---------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Kickoff** | 30 min | Virtual | Commitment + homework |
| **Session 1** | 3 hours | In-Person | Current state + 4 strategic themes |
| **Session 2** | 2 hours | Virtual | 2030 vision + sequencing |
| **Session 3** | 3 hours | In-Person | Strategy-on-a-Page draft |
| **Session 4** | 2 hours | Virtual | Final strategy + 90-day plan |

**Total Meeting Time:** 10.5 hours (6 hours in-person, 4.5 virtual)
**Total Homework:** ~5-8 hours across 4 packages
**Total Investment:** ~15-18 hours per participant

### Why This Structure Works

**Addresses Urgency ✅**
- Only 3-4 weeks start to finish
- Hard stop at 4 sessions
- No endless discussion cycles

**Balances Depth + Speed ✅**
- Homework creates readiness
- Sessions make decisions
- In-person for hard conversations
- Virtual for refinement

**Manages Two Constituencies ✅**
- Shared homework = shared language
- Role-specific questions honor differences
- Mixed AMC/client in all activities

**Produces Usable Artifacts ✅**
- Strategy-on-a-Page = communication tool
- 90-day action plan = execution roadmap
- Governance calendar = accountability

## 📋 HOMEWORK PACKAGES (4 Total)

### Homework #1: 2030 Assumptions + Reflection (2-3 hours)
**When:** After kickoff, before Session 1

**What:**
- Read 2030 assumptions with verified sources (8 major trends)
- Answer reflection questions (shared + role-specific)
- Respond to 2 provocative scenarios

**Scenarios Include:**
- AI Displacement: 70% of tasks automated by 2028
- Generational Shift: 60% of members are Millennials/Gen Z by 2030
- Revenue Pressure: 25% membership decline in economic downturn
- Competitive Disruption: Tech company enters your space

**Output:** Written responses shared with all participants

### Homework #2: Theme Deep-Dive (1.5-2 hours)
**When:** After Session 1, before Session 2

**What:**
- Research 1-2 assigned strategic themes
- Find external examples and case studies
- Answer 6 strategic questions per theme
- Prepare 5-minute presentation

**Output:** Research slides + notes

### Homework #3: Strategy-on-a-Page Sections (1.5 hours)
**When:** After Session 2, before Session 3

**What:**
- Draft assigned section of Strategy-on-a-Page
- Use provided template
- Keep concise (bullets, not paragraphs)
- Reference research and discussions

**Output:** Draft section submitted to facilitator

### Homework #4: 90-Day Action Plan (1 hour)
**When:** After Session 3, before Session 4

**What:**
- Identify 2-3 initiatives you own/contribute to
- Break into 30/60/90-day milestones
- Define success criteria
- Identify resources and dependencies

**Output:** Personal action plan table

## 🗓️ SESSION BREAKDOWN

### Session 1: Current State Alignment (3 hours, In-Person)

**Objectives:**
1. Build shared understanding of current capabilities/gaps
2. Surface and resolve factual disagreements
3. Identify 4 strategic themes

**Key Activities:**
- Homework share-out in mixed groups
- Current state assessment (Stop/Start/Continue)
- Strategic theme brainstorming and prioritization

**Output:** 4 prioritized strategic themes

### Session 2: 2030 Vision + Theme Development (2 hours, Virtual)

**Objectives:**
1. Develop 2030 vision for each theme
2. Identify interdependencies
3. Begin sequencing

**Key Activities:**
- Theme research presentations
- Integration discussion (dependencies, sequencing)
- Trade-off analysis

**Output:** Vision statements + dependency map + preliminary roadmap

### Session 3: Strategy-on-a-Page Development (3 hours, In-Person)

**Objectives:**
1. Refine and finalize Strategy-on-a-Page
2. Ensure consistency and feasibility
3. Build consensus

**Key Activities:**
- Section-by-section review and refinement
- Reality check (budget, timeline, capabilities)
- Visual design and one-page test

**Output:** Near-final Strategy-on-a-Page

### Session 4: Finalization + Accountability (2 hours, Virtual)

**Objectives:**
1. Sign off on final Strategy-on-a-Page
2. Lock in 90-day action plan
3. Establish governance

**Key Activities:**
- Final review and approval
- 90-day action plan synthesis
- Governance framework setup
- Commitment circle

**Output:** Final strategy + action plan + governance calendar

## ⚠️ RISKS AND MITIGATION

### Risk #1: Low Homework Completion
**Mitigation:**
- Kickoff call builds personal commitment
- Senior leader reinforces importance
- Reminder emails at strategic intervals
- Share completed homework (peer pressure)

### Risk #2: AMC-Client Misalignment
**Mitigation:**
- Surface in Session 1 (low stakes)
- Use research to arbitrate facts
- Client voice = primary for member decisions
- Escalation path defined upfront

### Risk #3: Too Abstract, Not Actionable
**Mitigation:**
- Force specificity (numbers, dates, examples)
- Reality check in Session 3
- 90-day action plan prevents shelf-ware
- Monthly accountability check-ins

### Risk #4: Loss of Momentum Post-Program
**Mitigation:**
- Governance built INTO program
- Calendar monthly check-ins before Session 4
- Executive sponsor owns follow-through
- 30-day review as "finish line"

### Risk #5: Scope Creep (>4 Sessions)
**Mitigation:**
- Hard stop at 4 sessions
- Parking lot for future topics
- Facilitator enforces time
- Separate program if more needed
