# ISO 27001 Documentation Requirements

## Overview

ISO 27001 requires specific documented information. This guide provides a complete checklist of all mandatory and recommended documentation for your ISMS.

**Documentation Principle**: "Fit for purpose" - documents should be appropriate for your organization's size and complexity. Don't over-document.

## Document Structure and Format

### Policy Structure Template

```
[Policy Name]

1. Purpose
   - Why this policy exists
   - What it aims to achieve

2. Scope
   - What/who this policy applies to
   - Any exclusions

3. Policy Statements
   - High-level requirements
   - Management commitments
   - 5-10 key principles

4. Roles and Responsibilities
   - Who is responsible for what

5. Compliance
   - Consequences of non-compliance
   - Reference to disciplinary process

6. Related Documents
   - Related policies, procedures, standards

7. Review and Approval
   - Review frequency (annually)
   - Approval authority (CEO, Board)
   - Version history

[Executive Signature]        [Date]
```

**Length**: 2-4 pages typical

---

### Procedure Structure Template

```
[Procedure Name]

1. Purpose
   - What this procedure covers

2. Scope
   - When to use this procedure
   - Who should follow it

3. Definitions
   - Key terms used in procedure

4. Roles and Responsibilities
   - Who does what in this process

5. Procedure Steps
   Step 1: [Action]
   - Details, tools, inputs/outputs

   Step 2: [Action]
   - Details, tools, inputs/outputs

   [Continue for all steps]

6. Related Documents
   - Policies, forms, templates

7. Revision History
   - Version, date, changes

Approved by: [Name]           [Date]
```

**Length**: 3-8 pages typical

## Document Control

### Version Control

All ISMS documents must have:
- Version number (e.g., v1.0, v1.1, v2.0)
- Date of approval
- Approval authority (who approved)
- Revision history (what changed)

**Versioning Scheme**:
- Major changes (v1.0 -> v2.0): Significant policy/procedure changes
- Minor changes (v1.0 -> v1.1): Clarifications, corrections, minor updates

---

### Document Repository

Store all ISMS documents in controlled location:
- **SharePoint/OneDrive** (permissions-based access)
- **Google Drive** (with access controls)
- **ISMS Platform** (Vanta, Drata, etc.)
- **Document Management System**

**Access Control**:
- Public documents: All employees can read
- Internal documents: Employees can read, ISMS Manager can edit
- Confidential documents: Restricted to ISMS team, management

---

### Review and Approval

**Review Frequency**:
- Policies: Annually or when significant changes
- Procedures: Annually or when process changes
- Records: Retention per policy (typically 1-7 years)

**Approval Process**:
1. Draft document (ISMS Manager, process owner)
2. Review (stakeholders, legal if needed)
3. Approval (CEO for policies, ISMS Manager for procedures)
4. Communication (notify all affected parties)
5. Training (if new or significant change)
6. Implementation (effective date)

## Documentation Tools

### Option 1: Manual (Spreadsheets and SharePoint)

**Pros**: Low cost, full control, simple
**Cons**: Manual effort, difficult to scale, no automation

**Setup**:
- SharePoint or Google Drive for document storage
- Excel/Sheets for risk register, asset inventory, records tracking
- Word/Docs for policies and procedures

**Cost**: $0-$2,000 (mostly labor)

---

### Option 2: ISMS Platform (Vanta, Drata, Secureframe, etc.)

**Pros**: Automation, evidence collection, compliance tracking, audit-ready
**Cons**: Cost, vendor dependency

**Features**:
- Policy and procedure templates
- Automated evidence collection (integrates with cloud, HR, etc.)
- Risk management module
- Audit management
- Employee training tracking
- Continuous compliance monitoring
- Reporting and dashboards

**Cost**: $10,000-$30,000/year (depending on platform and features)

**Recommendation for Your Organization**: Consider ISMS platform in Year 1 to accelerate implementation and reduce manual effort. ROI is strong if you value time to certification.

---

### Option 3: Hybrid

**Approach**: Use platform for automation, supplement with custom documentation where needed

**Cost**: Platform cost + labor for custom docs

- Records collection: 1-2 days/month (or automated with ISMS platform)

## Certification Audit: What Auditors Will Review

### Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review)

Auditors will review:
- [ ] ISMS scope
- [ ] Information security policy
- [ ] Risk assessment methodology and results
- [ ] Risk treatment plan
- [ ] Statement of Applicability (SOA)
- [ ] Information security objectives
- [ ] All relevant policies and procedures
- [ ] Organizational structure (roles and responsibilities)
- [ ] Competence records (training)

**Common Stage 1 Findings**:
- Incomplete SOA (missing justifications)
- Risk assessment doesn't cover all assets
- Policies missing required elements
- Objectives not measurable
- Documentation doesn't match actual practices

---

### Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Review)

Auditors will verify:
- [ ] Controls are actually implemented (not just documented)
- [ ] Evidence of implementation (records, logs, configurations)
- [ ] Training has been delivered
- [ ] Incidents are being managed
- [ ] Access controls are enforced
- [ ] Backups are being performed and tested
- [ ] Monitoring is operational
- [ ] Management review has occurred
- [ ] Internal audit has been conducted
- [ ] Corrective actions are tracked and closed

**Evidence Auditors Will Sample**:
- Access request approvals
- Training records
- Incident reports
- Backup logs and restore tests
- Vulnerability scan reports
- Internal audit report
- Management review minutes
- Cloud security configurations (screenshots, logs)

## Documentation Red Flags (What Auditors Dislike)

**Avoid**:
1. **Copy-paste from internet** without customization (obvious and fails audit)
2. **Excessive documentation** (100+ page policies no one reads)
3. **Documentation doesn't match reality** (document says one thing, you do another)
4. **Generic templates** (no company name, no specific details)
5. **Outdated documents** (old dates, superseded versions in use)
6. **No version control** (can't tell which is current version)
7. **Unapproved documents** (no executive signature, no approval date)
8. **Inconsistencies** (policy says one thing, procedure says another)

**Auditors Look For**:
1. Evidence-based (you can prove what you documented)
2. Appropriate to your organization (right-sized, not over/under-documented)
3. Current and maintained (regular reviews, up-to-date)
4. Consistent (all documents align)
5. Practiced (you actually follow your documented procedures)
